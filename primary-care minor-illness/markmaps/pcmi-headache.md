[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="101" title="Overview" color="green" step="1" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.101" role="button">Overview</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Clinical challenge:</strong> Identify dangerous secondary headache quickly while still managing migraine, tension-type headache, cluster headache, and medication-overuse headache well.</li> <li><strong>Guideline frame:</strong> NICE CG150 plus same-day pathways for thunderclap headache, meningitis, stroke, and giant cell arteritis.</li> <li><strong>Core stance:</strong> Headache diagnosis is mainly made by careful history, not reflex imaging.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="102" title="Pathophysiology" color="green" step="2" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.102" role="button">Pathophysiology/basic science</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Migraine:</strong> neurovascular disorder involving trigeminovascular activation and CGRP.</li> <li><strong>Tension-type headache:</strong> often reflects myofascial pain and central sensitisation.</li> <li><strong>Cluster headache:</strong> hypothalamic and trigeminal-autonomic activation.</li> </ul></details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis (ddx) framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> SAH, meningitis, stroke / bleed, GCA, raised ICP.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Common primary headaches:</strong> migraine, tension-type, cluster, medication-overuse headache.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Other causes:</strong> sinus, cervicogenic, hypertensive emergency context.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">History taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Onset and time course</strong>: thunderclap, progressive, recurrent, or changing?</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Phenotype and associated symptoms</strong>: site, character, severity, aura, nausea, autonomic features.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Red flag screen</strong>: fever, meningism, focal neurology, visual change, jaw claudication, cancer, immunosuppression.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="104.4"][/sc]<strong>Medication use and frequency</strong>: assess for medication-overuse headache.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>General assessment and vital signs</strong></li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>Neurological examination and fundoscopy</strong></li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>Head and neck examination</strong>: meningism, temporal arteries, cranial tenderness, TMJ / neck.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Emergency:</strong> thunderclap headache, seizure, reduced consciousness, focal neurology, or suspected meningitis.</li> <li><strong>Same-day urgent assessment:</strong> suspected GCA, papilloedema, rapidly progressive new headache, or first likely cluster presentation needing confirmation.</li> <li><strong>Discipline point:</strong> a “known migraine” label does not safely explain a changed pattern.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="107" title="Investigations" color="yellow" step="7" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.107" role="button">Investigations strategy &amp; interpretation</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>No routine imaging</strong> for classic primary headache without red flags.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>ESR / CRP</strong> when GCA is suspected.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Fundoscopy and targeted bedside checks</strong> matter more than indiscriminate testing.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="108"][/sc]NICE CG150 emphasises diagnosis by history.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="108"][/sc]Routine imaging is not helpful for typical primary headache.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="108"][/sc]Medication-overuse headache should be explained early and often.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="108"][/sc]Triptans are contraindicated in IHD, prior stroke/TIA, and uncontrolled hypertension.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="109" title="Diagnostic Timeout" color="pink" step="9" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.109" role="button">Diagnostic timeout &amp; cognitive biases</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Pause:</strong> Is this actually a new dangerous headache wearing an old “migraine” label?</li> <li><strong>Check:</strong> Have I excluded GCA, raised ICP, meningitis, and SAH?</li> <li><strong>Bias risk:</strong> Anchoring on familiar headache labels and missing change in pattern.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Migraine:</strong> early acute treatment, trigger review, and headache diary.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Tension-type headache:</strong> simple analgesia plus sleep, hydration, stress, and musculoskeletal review.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Cluster pattern:</strong> confirm and link into urgent treatment pathways rather than treating as a routine “bad headache”.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Medication-overuse headache:</strong> explain, withdraw overused medication, and arrange review.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="111" title="Special Populations" color="blue" step="11" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.111" role="button">Special populations and role-aware notes</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Older adults:</strong> treat new headache as secondary until assessed otherwise.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Pregnancy:</strong> paracetamol first-line, but management depends on context and gestation.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Children and role-aware care:</strong> lower threshold for escalation if pattern is unusual, persistent, or hard to classify confidently.</li> </ul> </details>
