[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="101" title="Overview" color="green" step="1" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.101" role="button">Overview</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Clinical challenge:</strong> identify dangerous secondary headache quickly while still managing migraine, tension-type headache, cluster headache, and medication-overuse headache well.</li> <li><strong>Guideline frame:</strong> NICE CG150 plus same-day pathways for thunderclap headache, meningitis, stroke, and giant cell arteritis.</li> <li><strong>Core stance:</strong> headache diagnosis is mainly made by careful history, not reflex imaging.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="102" title="Pathophysiology" color="green" step="2" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.102" role="button">Pathophysiology/basic science</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Migraine:</strong> neurovascular disorder involving trigeminovascular activation and CGRP.</li> <li><strong>Tension-type headache:</strong> often reflects myofascial pain and central sensitisation.</li> <li><strong>Cluster headache:</strong> hypothalamic and trigeminal-autonomic activation.</li> <li><strong>Secondary headache:</strong> may reflect bleeding, infection, raised ICP, vasculitis, or medication overuse, so the history must actively search for danger signs.</li> </ul></details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis (ddx) framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> SAH, meningitis, stroke / bleed, GCA, raised ICP.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Common primary headaches:</strong> migraine, tension-type, cluster, medication-overuse headache.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Other causes:</strong> sinus disease, cervicogenic / TMJ headache, hypertensive emergency context.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">History taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Onset and time course</strong>: thunderclap, progressive, recurrent, or changing?</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Phenotype and associated symptoms</strong>: site, character, severity, aura, nausea, autonomic features.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Red flag screen</strong>: fever, meningism, focal neurology, visual change, jaw claudication, trauma, cancer, immunosuppression.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="104.4"][/sc]<strong>Medication use and frequency</strong>: assess for medication-overuse headache.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>General assessment and vital signs</strong></li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>Neurological examination and fundoscopy</strong></li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>Head and neck examination</strong>: meningism, temporal arteries, cranial tenderness, TMJ / neck.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Emergency:</strong> thunderclap headache, seizure, reduced consciousness, focal neurology, suspected meningitis, or concerning post-traumatic features.</li> <li><strong>Same-day urgent assessment:</strong> suspected GCA, papilloedema, rapidly progressive new headache, new headache in an immunocompromised or cancer patient, or a first likely cluster presentation needing confirmation.</li> <li><strong>Routine primary-care management:</strong> stable recurrent migraine or tension-type headache with normal examination and no red flags.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="107" title="Investigations" color="yellow" step="7" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.107" role="button">Investigations strategy &amp; interpretation</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>No routine neuroimaging</strong> for classic primary headache without red flags.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>ESR / CRP</strong> when GCA is suspected, but do not delay action if vision is at risk.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Pregnancy test / BP / fundoscopy</strong> are high-yield bedside decision points.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Urgent neuroimaging pathway</strong> when secondary headache is suspected clinically.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="108"][/sc]NICE CG150 emphasises diagnosis by history and discourages routine imaging for typical primary headache.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="108"][/sc]Medication-overuse headache should be explained proactively to anyone relying on frequent acute treatment.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="108"][/sc]For migraine, acute therapy works best early in the attack; prophylaxis is for frequent or disabling headaches.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="108"][/sc]Triptans are contraindicated in IHD, prior stroke/TIA, and uncontrolled hypertension.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="109" title="Diagnostic Timeout" color="pink" step="9" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.109" role="button">Diagnostic timeout &amp; cognitive biases</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Do not normalise a new headache in older adults:</strong> always think GCA and secondary causes.</li> <li><strong>Do not confuse chronic analgesic use with treatment failure alone:</strong> think MOH.</li> <li><strong>Do not let a migraine history blind you:</strong> patients with known migraine can still have a new dangerous headache.</li> <li><strong>Do not skip fundoscopy when raised ICP is in play.</strong></li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Migraine:</strong> early acute treatment, consider triptan plus simple analgesia where appropriate, and discuss triggers and headache diary.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Tension-type headache:</strong> simple analgesia plus sleep, hydration, stress, and musculoskeletal review.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Cluster pattern:</strong> confirm urgently and link into same-day or early specialist treatment pathways rather than treating as a routine bad headache.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Medication-overuse headache:</strong> explain, plan withdrawal, and arrange review.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Urgent return:</strong> thunderclap onset, fever with neck stiffness, new weakness, seizure, visual loss, or major change in headache pattern.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1275" anchor="111" title="Special Populations" color="blue" step="11" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.111" role="button">Special populations and role-aware notes</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Children and adolescents:</strong> migraine is common, but persistent or unusual headache deserves a lower threshold for escalation.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Older adults:</strong> treat new headache as secondary until assessed otherwise; GCA is a recurring must-not-miss.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Pregnancy:</strong> paracetamol is first-line, but medication choice depends on gestation and comorbidity.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1275" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Role-aware note:</strong> pharmacist prescribers can manage clear primary headache presentations within competence, but abnormal neurology, GCA features, or diagnostic uncertainty should move to same-day GP or ED assessment.</li> </ul> </details>
