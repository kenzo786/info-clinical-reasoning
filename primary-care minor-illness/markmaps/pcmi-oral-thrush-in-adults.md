[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="101" title="Overview" color="green" step="1" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.101" role="button">Overview</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Commonly due to Candida overgrowth</strong>, but fixed lesions need a different pathway.</li> <li><strong>Main task:</strong> separate scrapable thrush from leukoplakia, lichen planus, oral hairy leukoplakia, and cancer.</li> <li><strong>Core stance:</strong> treat straightforward candidiasis, correct the trigger, and refer if the lesion is not classic.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="102" title="Pathophysiology" color="green" step="2" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.102" role="button">Pathophysiology</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Candidate triggers:</strong> inhaled steroids, antibiotics, dentures, diabetes, immunosuppression.</li> <li><strong>Typical lesions:</strong> creamy-white plaques that scrape off.</li> <li><strong>Why it matters:</strong> reducing the trigger reduces recurrence.</li> </ul></details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Common:</strong> thrush, food debris, denture stomatitis.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Often confused with:</strong> leukoplakia, lichen planus, oral hairy leukoplakia.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> squamous cell carcinoma and oesophageal spread.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">Focused history taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Can it be wiped off?</strong> fixed lesions need urgent review.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Triggers:</strong> inhaled steroids, antibiotics, dentures, diabetes, immunosuppression.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Symptom screen:</strong> soreness, altered taste, odynophagia, dysphagia, weight loss, fever.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="104.4"][/sc]<strong>Cancer screen:</strong> smoking, alcohol, ulcer, bleeding, neck lump, induration.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Focused examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>Oral inspection</strong>: scrapable plaques support thrush.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>Dentures and inhalers</strong>: check overnight removal, cleaning, and rinse-spit technique.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>General exam if indicated</strong>: weight loss, fever, lymph nodes, systemic illness.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Urgent referral:</strong> non-scrapable patch, ulcer, induration, bleeding, neck lump, weight loss.</li> <li><strong>Same-day review:</strong> odynophagia, dysphagia, fever, or significant immunosuppression.</li> <li><strong>Routine care only:</strong> classic scrapable thrush with an obvious trigger.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="107" title="Investigations" color="yellow" step="7" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.107" role="button">Investigations and decision points</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>No routine tests</strong> for classic uncomplicated cases.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Consider swab / review</strong> if atypical, recurrent, or treatment failure.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Consider blood tests</strong> if recurrent disease suggests diabetes or immunosuppression.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="108"][/sc]Topical antifungals are usually first line.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="108"][/sc]Miconazole can interact with warfarin and some statins.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="108"][/sc]Nystatin is a safer option in polypharmacy.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1297" anchor="108"][/sc]Address inhaler technique and denture hygiene to reduce recurrence.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="109" title="Diagnostic Timeout" color="pink" step="9" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.109" role="button">Diagnostic timeout</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Pause:</strong> does this lesion really behave like thrush?</li> <li><strong>Check:</strong> is it scrapable, or is it fixed / ulcerated / indurated?</li> <li><strong>Bias risk:</strong> repeating treatment when the lesion is actually a mimic.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Uncomplicated thrush:</strong> topical antifungal plus oral hygiene and trigger correction.</li> <li><strong>Severe / recurrent disease:</strong> review for diabetes, inhaled steroid use, denture factors, or immunosuppression.</li> <li><strong>Safety-net:</strong> return for odynophagia, dysphagia, weight loss, bleeding, or persistent lesion.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="111" title="Special Populations" color="blue" step="11" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.111" role="button">Special populations and role-aware notes</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Immunosuppressed adults:</strong> lower threshold for systemic treatment or broader work-up.</li> <li><strong>Denture wearers:</strong> overnight removal and cleaning matter.</li> <li><strong>Inhaled steroid users:</strong> rinse and spit after use.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1297" anchor="112" title="Cases" color="grey" step="12" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-grey" open> <summary id="s-is.112" role="button">Illustrative cases</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Case 1:</strong> scrapable plaques in a denture wearer can usually be managed as thrush.</li> <li><strong>Case 2:</strong> recurrent thrush on inhaled steroids needs technique review and trigger correction.</li> <li><strong>Case 3:</strong> a fixed lesion with weight loss or a neck lump needs urgent assessment.</li> </ul> </details>
