[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="101" title="Overview" color="green" step="1" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.101" role="button">Overview</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Pattern:</strong> fewer bowel movements, hard stools, straining, bloating, incomplete evacuation.</li> <li><strong>Main job:</strong> separate functional constipation from impaction, medication effects, and red-flag disease.</li> <li><strong>Core stance:</strong> manage simple cases stepwise, but do not miss serious pathology.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="102" title="Pathophysiology" color="green" step="2" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.102" role="button">Pathophysiology/basic science</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Slow transit:</strong> too much water is absorbed, so stool becomes hard.</li> <li><strong>Defecatory disorder:</strong> pelvic floor / sphincter coordination is poor.</li> <li><strong>Why it matters:</strong> medicines, dehydration, immobility, and metabolic disease often contribute.</li> </ul></details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Common and likely:</strong> functional constipation, drug-induced constipation, IBS-C.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Often confused with constipation:</strong> faecal impaction, hypothyroidism, medication side effect.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> colorectal cancer, bowel obstruction, rectal / abdominal mass.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">History taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Symptom pattern</strong>: frequency, straining, hard stools, bloating, incomplete evacuation.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Red flag screen</strong>: bleeding, weight loss, anaemia, vomiting, pain, distension, older age with new change.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Medication history</strong>: opioids, anticholinergics, iron, calcium channel blockers, antidepressants.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="104.4"][/sc]<strong>Functional context</strong>: fibre, fluids, mobility, toileting routine, pregnancy, neurological disease.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>When exam is needed</strong>: pain, vomiting, distension, suspected impaction, or cancer concern.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>Abdomen</strong>: distension, tenderness, mass, bowel sounds.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>DRE when indicated</strong>: impaction, tone, mass, rectal loading.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="105.4"][/sc]<strong>General observation</strong>: pallor, weight loss, frailty, confusion, dehydration.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Urgent same-day:</strong> severe abdominal pain, vomiting, distension, or suspected obstruction.</li> <li><strong>Prompt GP / 2WW concern:</strong> bleeding, weight loss, anaemia, new bowel habit change in older adult, or a mass.</li> <li><strong>Prompt review:</strong> faecal impaction, overflow diarrhoea, confusion, or failure of sensible OTC treatment.</li> <li><strong>Do not treat as simple constipation:</strong> if the history is atypical or the patient appears systemically unwell.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="107" title="Investigations" color="yellow" step="7" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.107" role="button">Investigations strategy &amp; interpretation</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>No routine test:</strong> typical uncomplicated constipation may be managed clinically.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Blood tests:</strong> if anaemia, hypothyroidism, or metabolic disease is suspected.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>DRE / imaging:</strong> if impaction or obstruction is suspected.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Urgent cancer pathway:</strong> if red flags suggest colorectal cancer.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="108"][/sc]Stepwise care starts with lifestyle advice.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="108"][/sc]Macrogols are usually the preferred osmotic option for hard stools.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="108"][/sc]Stimulants are short-term rescue treatment, not indefinite therapy.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="108"][/sc]Faecal impaction needs a separate plan from routine constipation.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="109" title="Diagnostic Timeout" color="pink" step="9" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.109" role="button">Diagnostic timeout &amp; cognitive biases</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Do not ignore red flags because constipation is common.</strong></li> <li><strong>Do not miss impaction in older or immobile patients.</strong></li> <li><strong>Do not keep repeating stimulant laxatives without review.</strong></li> <li><strong>Do not overlook a drug cause when symptoms started after a new medicine.</strong></li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Core management:</strong> diet, fluids, exercise, toileting routine, then stepwise laxatives.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Impaction:</strong> use a separate disimpaction plan rather than routine dose escalation.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Follow-up:</strong> review if symptoms do not settle after a sensible trial.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Safety-netting:</strong> urgent help for bleeding, weight loss, severe pain, vomiting, or abdominal swelling.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="111" title="Special Populations" color="blue" step="11" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.111" role="button">Special populations and role-aware notes</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Children:</strong> osmotic laxatives are often preferred and persistent symptoms deserve careful review.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Older adults:</strong> impaction and drug causes are common, and confusion may be a clue.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Pregnancy / breastfeeding:</strong> conservative measures first; bulk-forming and osmotic laxatives are usually acceptable.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Role-aware note:</strong> simple constipation can be managed in pharmacy, but red flags, impaction, or persistence need GP assessment.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1293" anchor="112" title="Illustrative Cases" color="grey" step="12" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-grey" open> <summary id="s-is.112" role="button">Illustrative cases</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="112"][/sc]<strong>Case 1:</strong> hard stools, bloating, low fibre intake: functional constipation.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="112"][/sc]<strong>Case 2:</strong> overflow diarrhoea and confusion: faecal impaction until proven otherwise.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1293" anchor="112"][/sc]<strong>Case 3:</strong> constipation with weight loss and bleeding: urgent colorectal cancer referral.</li> </ul> </details>
