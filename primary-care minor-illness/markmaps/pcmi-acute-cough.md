[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1272" anchor="101" title="Overview" color="green" step="1" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.101" role="button">Overview</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Clinical challenge:</strong> Most acute cough is viral, but the consultation must actively exclude pneumonia, sepsis, PE, pneumothorax, acute asthma/COPD, and heart failure.</li> <li><strong>Guideline frame:</strong> NICE NG120 plus CRB-65 when pneumonia is suspected.</li> <li><strong>Core stance:</strong> Start with physiology and red flags before deciding this is uncomplicated infection.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1272" anchor="102" title="Pathophysiology" color="green" step="2" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.102" role="button">Pathophysiology/basic science</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Viral URTI / bronchitis:</strong> airway inflammation and hyper-responsiveness drive cough.</li> <li><strong>Pneumonia:</strong> alveolar infection produces fever, pleuritic pain, and focal chest signs.</li> <li><strong>Why it matters:</strong> new cough can be the surface clue to a more dangerous cardiopulmonary process.</li> </ul></details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1272" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis (ddx) framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> pneumonia, sepsis, PE, pneumothorax, acute asthma/COPD, heart failure.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Common causes:</strong> viral URTI, acute bronchitis, post-viral cough.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Easily missed:</strong> ACE inhibitor cough, pertussis, foreign body, lung cancer context.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1272" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">History taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Duration and trajectory</strong>: improving, static, or worsening?</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Breathlessness and exercise tolerance</strong>: rest breathlessness changes the risk picture.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Chest pain and haemoptysis</strong>: pleuritic pain or blood in sputum needs escalation thinking.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="104.4"][/sc]<strong>Systemic features and comorbidity</strong>: fever, confusion, COPD/asthma, heart failure, cancer, immunosuppression, VTE risk.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1272" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>General impression and respiratory effort</strong></li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>Vital signs</strong>: temperature, pulse, respiratory rate, BP, SpO2.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>Chest examination</strong>: air entry, wheeze, crackles, focal dullness.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1272" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Emergency / same-day:</strong> severe breathlessness, hypoxia, confusion, hypotension, cyanosis, haemoptysis, or suspected sepsis.</li> <li><strong>Urgent community review:</strong> suspected pneumonia or deterioration in a frail or immunocompromised patient.</li> <li><strong>Diagnostic discipline:</strong> do not label “bronchitis” before the physiology has been checked.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1272" anchor="107" title="Investigations" color="yellow" step="7" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.107" role="button">Investigations strategy &amp; interpretation</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>CRB-65</strong> when pneumonia is suspected.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Pulse oximetry</strong> is high-value in breathless or comorbid patients.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Chest X-ray</strong> when pneumonia, malignancy, or structural disease is suspected.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1272" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="108"][/sc]NICE NG120: do not routinely offer antibiotics for uncomplicated acute cough.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="108"][/sc]Explain early that cough often lasts 3 to 4 weeks.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="108"][/sc]Do not use sputum colour alone to justify antibiotics.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="108"][/sc]Pholcodine is withdrawn; codeine linctus is POM-only.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1272" anchor="109" title="Diagnostic Timeout" color="pink" step="9" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.109" role="button">Diagnostic timeout &amp; cognitive biases</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Pause:</strong> Could this be PE, pneumothorax, or heart failure rather than “just bronchitis”?</li> <li><strong>Check:</strong> Have I documented the physiology properly?</li> <li><strong>Bias risk:</strong> Overcalling bacterial infection because the patient sounds unwell or has coloured sputum.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1272" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Low-risk viral cough:</strong> self-care, smoking cessation, expectation management, and clear return advice.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Suspected pneumonia:</strong> CRB-65, local antimicrobial guidance, and place-of-care decision.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Urgent return:</strong> worsening breathlessness, chest pain, haemoptysis, confusion, or failure to improve as expected.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1272" anchor="111" title="Special Populations" color="blue" step="11" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.111" role="button">Special populations and role-aware notes</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Children:</strong> escalate sooner if feeding, work of breathing, or foreign body concerns are present.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Older adults:</strong> confusion or functional decline may be the clue to pneumonia.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1272" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Pregnancy and role-aware care:</strong> use pregnancy-safe symptom advice and escalate beyond pharmacy scope when assessment uncertainty remains.</li> </ul> </details>
