[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="101" title="Overview" color="green" step="1" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.101" role="button">Overview</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Tinea pedis:</strong> common superficial fungal infection of the feet.</li> <li><strong>Main task:</strong> separate routine fungal disease from infection, diabetic foot risk, and inflammatory mimics.</li> <li><strong>Typical pattern:</strong> itchy scaling or maceration between the toes, especially the fourth and fifth.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="102" title="Pathophysiology" color="green" step="2" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.102" role="button">Pathophysiology</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Cause:</strong> dermatophyte fungi in warm, moist, occlusive conditions.</li> <li><strong>Barrier damage:</strong> maceration and fissures increase discomfort and infection risk.</li> <li><strong>Why it matters:</strong> the rash may be minor, but the foot may not be low risk.</li> </ul></details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Common:</strong> interdigital, moccasin-type, or vesicular tinea pedis.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Often confused with:</strong> eczema, psoriasis, erythrasma, pitted keratolysis.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> cellulitis, diabetic foot breakdown, nail-related recurrent fungal disease.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">Focused history taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Symptom pattern</strong>: itch, soreness, fissures, blisters, toe webs vs soles.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Time course and prior treatment</strong>: recurrence, failed OTCs, adherence.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Risk factors</strong>: occlusive footwear, sport, communal showers, sweaty feet, nail disease.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="104.4"][/sc]<strong>Complication screen</strong>: diabetes, PVD, immunosuppression, spreading redness, fever, skin breakdown.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Focused examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>Distribution</strong>: toe webs, soles, heels, sides of feet, bilateral vs unilateral.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>Morphology</strong>: scale, maceration, fissures, vesicles, warmth, tenderness.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>Nails and skin integrity</strong>: onychomycosis, deep cracks, ulcers, breakdown.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Urgent:</strong> painful spreading erythema, fever, swelling, suspected cellulitis.</li> <li><strong>Prompt review:</strong> ulceration, immunocompromise, poorly controlled diabetes, extensive disease, diagnostic uncertainty.</li> <li><strong>Routine topical care only:</strong> classic uncomplicated tinea with no high-risk foot features.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="107" title="Investigations" color="yellow" step="7" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.107" role="button">Investigations and decision points</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>No routine test</strong> for classic cases.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Mycology</strong> if diagnosis is uncertain or treatment fails.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Nail review</strong> if recurrence suggests onychomycosis.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Foot-risk assessment</strong> if diabetes or skin breakdown is present.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="108"][/sc]Topical antifungals are first line for uncomplicated tinea pedis.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="108"][/sc]Topical terbinafine often has a shorter course than imidazoles.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="108"][/sc]Moccasin-type or refractory disease is less likely to settle with simple topical therapy alone.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1300" anchor="108"][/sc]Do not use a plain topical steroid on suspected fungal disease.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="109" title="Diagnostic Timeout" color="pink" step="9" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.109" role="button">Diagnostic timeout</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Pause:</strong> is this definitely fungal rather than eczema or psoriasis?</li> <li><strong>Check:</strong> is there pain, spreading erythema, or diabetic foot breakdown?</li> <li><strong>Bias risk:</strong> treating recurrent disease without checking for nail involvement.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Core treatment:</strong> topical antifungal plus foot hygiene and drying advice.</li> <li><strong>Counselling:</strong> dry between the toes, change socks daily, reduce occlusive footwear, use communal-area footwear.</li> <li><strong>Safety-net:</strong> seek review if the foot becomes painful, hot, swollen, ulcerated, or fails to improve.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="111" title="Special Populations" color="blue" step="11" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.111" role="button">Special populations and role-aware notes</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Children:</strong> product licences vary and athlete's foot is less common.</li> <li><strong>Pregnancy / breastfeeding:</strong> topical imidazoles are usually the simplest option.</li> <li><strong>Older adults:</strong> consider self-care ability, polypharmacy, and foot-risk comorbidity.</li> <li><strong>Diabetes / immunocompromise:</strong> lower threshold for medical review.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1300" anchor="112" title="Cases" color="grey" step="12" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-grey" open> <summary id="s-is.112" role="button">Illustrative cases</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Case 1:</strong> swimmer with itchy toe-web maceration: uncomplicated tinea pedis.</li> <li><strong>Case 2:</strong> diabetic patient with fissures and redness: higher-risk foot problem needing review.</li> <li><strong>Case 3:</strong> persistent sole scaling with thickened nails: think recurrent fungal reservoir or mimic.</li> </ul> </details>
