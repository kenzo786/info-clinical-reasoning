[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1288" anchor="101" title="Overview" color="green" step="1" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.101" role="button">Overview</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Pattern:</strong> intense nocturnal itch with contact spread and classic sites.</li> <li><strong>Main job:</strong> confirm the diagnosis, exclude crusted scabies / infection, and prevent reinfestation.</li> <li><strong>Core stance:</strong> treat the patient and close contacts together.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1288" anchor="102" title="Pathophysiology" color="green" step="2" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.102" role="button">Pathophysiology/basic science</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Mite:</strong> <em>Sarcoptes scabiei var. hominis</em> burrows in the stratum corneum.</li> <li><strong>Symptoms:</strong> delayed type IV hypersensitivity to mite material.</li> <li><strong>Why it matters:</strong> itch can continue after successful treatment.</li> </ul></details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1288" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Common and likely:</strong> scabies, eczema, insect bites.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Often confused with scabies:</strong> dermatitis herpetiformis, folliculitis, urticaria.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> crusted scabies, secondary bacterial infection, outbreak setting.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1288" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">History taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Itch pattern</strong>: severe nocturnal itch and sleep disturbance.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Contact history</strong>: household members, partners, close skin-to-skin contacts, outbreaks.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Distribution</strong>: finger webs, wrists, axillae, waistline, genitalia, excoriations, burrows.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="104.4"][/sc]<strong>Risk context</strong>: immunosuppression, care home exposure, previous scabicide use, incorrect application.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1288" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>When exam is needed</strong>: uncertainty, severe itch, contact spread, or possible complications.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>Classic sites</strong>: finger webs, wrists, elbows, axillae, waistline, buttocks, genitalia.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>Look for complications</strong>: crusting, thick scale, secondary infection, widespread excoriation.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="105.4"][/sc]<strong>Diagnostic aids</strong>: dermoscopy or burrow ink test if needed.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1288" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Urgent same-day:</strong> crusted scabies, widespread crusting or warty scale, or suspected outbreak in a care setting.</li> <li><strong>Urgent review:</strong> secondary bacterial infection, fever, pain, pus, or spreading redness.</li> <li><strong>Prompt referral:</strong> diagnostic uncertainty or failure after correctly applied treatment to all contacts.</li> <li><strong>Lower threshold:</strong> immunosuppression, frailty, or inability to manage treatment correctly at home.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1288" anchor="107" title="Investigations" color="yellow" step="7" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.107" role="button">Investigations strategy &amp; interpretation</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>No routine test:</strong> many cases are clinical diagnoses.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Dermoscopy / scraping:</strong> if the diagnosis is uncertain.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Infection work-up:</strong> if bacterial infection is suspected.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Public health input:</strong> if an institutional outbreak is suspected.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1288" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="108"][/sc]Treat contacts simultaneously.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="108"][/sc]Topical treatment needs two applications one week apart.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="108"][/sc]Post-treatment itch can persist for weeks.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="108"][/sc]Oral ivermectin is specialist-led for crusted, resistant, or outbreak cases.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1288" anchor="109" title="Diagnostic Timeout" color="pink" step="9" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.109" role="button">Diagnostic timeout &amp; cognitive biases</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Do not miss scabies because the rash looks like eczema.</strong></li> <li><strong>Do not forget to ask about itchy contacts.</strong></li> <li><strong>Do not treat contacts on different days.</strong></li> <li><strong>Do not assume ongoing itch always means active infestation.</strong></li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1288" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Core treatment:</strong> permethrin first line; malathion if needed.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Household measures:</strong> treat all contacts together and hot-wash / bag items.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Counselling:</strong> explain correct application, repeat dose, and post-treatment itch.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Safety-netting:</strong> review if new burrows appear or infection develops.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1288" anchor="111" title="Special Populations" color="blue" step="11" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.111" role="button">Special populations and role-aware notes</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Children:</strong> check age-appropriate licensing and product guidance.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Older adults:</strong> think crusted scabies early and ask for help with application.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Pregnancy / breastfeeding:</strong> permethrin is generally preferred first line.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1288" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Role-aware note:</strong> uncomplicated cases can be managed in pharmacy, but crusting, immunosuppression, outbreaks, or failed treatment need GP / specialist care.</li> </ul> </details>
