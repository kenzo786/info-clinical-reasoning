[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="101" title="Overview" color="green" step="1" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.101" role="button">Overview</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Clinical challenge:</strong> Most acute back pain is mechanical, but every consultation must actively exclude cauda equina, fracture, malignancy, infection, and AAA.</li> <li><strong>Guideline frame:</strong> NICE NG59 supports an activity-first, low-imaging approach.</li> <li><strong>Core stance:</strong> Pain severity alone does not define structural danger.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="102" title="Pathophysiology" color="green" step="2" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.102" role="button">Pathophysiology/basic science</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Mechanical pain:</strong> often arises from muscles, ligaments, facet joints, or discs without dangerous pathology.</li> <li><strong>Sciatica:</strong> neuropathic pain from lumbar nerve root irritation.</li> <li><strong>Why it matters:</strong> severe pain can still be benign, and serious pathology can hide behind a familiar story.</li> </ul></details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis (ddx) framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> cauda equina syndrome, vertebral fracture, spinal malignancy, spinal infection, AAA / visceral referred pain.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Common causes:</strong> non-specific low back pain, sciatica, muscle strain.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Alternative patterns:</strong> inflammatory, renal, pancreatic, or pelvic referred pain.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">History taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Pain behaviour</strong>: onset, trigger, site, radiation, aggravating and easing factors.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Cauda equina screen</strong>: saddle symptoms, urinary retention, incontinence, bilateral leg symptoms.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Fracture / cancer / infection red flags</strong>: trauma, steroids, osteoporosis, fever, weight loss, cancer history.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="104.4"][/sc]<strong>Function and psychosocial impact</strong>: mobility, sleep, work, fear, mood.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>Observation and movement</strong>: posture, gait, ability to mobilise.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>Spinal and neurological examination</strong>: tenderness, range of movement, power, reflexes, sensation.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>Straight leg raise and focused abdominal / systemic check</strong></li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Emergency:</strong> suspected cauda equina syndrome, rupturing AAA, or severe progressive neurology.</li> <li><strong>Same-day urgent review:</strong> suspected fracture, infection, malignancy, or rapidly worsening radiculopathy.</li> <li><strong>Discipline point:</strong> do not let a familiar “mechanical pain” story replace a proper red-flag screen.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="107" title="Investigations" color="yellow" step="7" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.107" role="button">Investigations strategy &amp; interpretation</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>No routine imaging</strong> for uncomplicated mechanical pain.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>MRI</strong> when cauda equina, infection, malignancy, or significant radiculopathy is suspected.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Blood tests</strong> if infection or malignancy is possible.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="108"][/sc]NICE NG59 advises against routine imaging without red flags.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="108"][/sc]Staying active is part of treatment, not an optional add-on.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="108"][/sc]Paracetamol alone is not recommended for low back pain.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="108"][/sc]Routine gabapentinoids, benzodiazepines, and opioid drift are poor practice for simple back pain.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="109" title="Diagnostic Timeout" color="pink" step="9" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.109" role="button">Diagnostic timeout &amp; cognitive biases</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Pause:</strong> Have I asked the bladder, bowel, and saddle questions clearly enough?</li> <li><strong>Check:</strong> Am I mistaking severe pain for dangerous pathology, or dangerous pathology for simple pain?</li> <li><strong>Bias risk:</strong> Anchoring on “mechanical pain” without a proper red-flag screen.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Low-risk pain:</strong> encourage activity, explain recovery, and use the lowest-risk analgesia that enables movement.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Sciatica:</strong> assess function and watch for neurological progression.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Urgent return:</strong> saddle numbness, bladder or bowel change, progressive weakness, fever, or worsening night pain.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="111" title="Special Populations" color="blue" step="11" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.111" role="button">Special populations and role-aware notes</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Children:</strong> lower threshold for medical assessment.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Older adults:</strong> think fracture, malignancy, and AAA earlier.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Pregnancy and role-aware care:</strong> prioritise non-pharmacological management and escalate if assessment uncertainty or red flags remain.</li> </ul> </details>
