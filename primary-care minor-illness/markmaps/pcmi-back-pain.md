[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis (ddx) framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> cauda equina, vertebral fracture, malignancy, spinal infection, AAA or other visceral referred pain.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Common causes:</strong> non-specific low back pain, sciatica or lumbar radiculopathy, muscle strain.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Alternative patterns:</strong> inflammatory back pain, renal colic, pyelonephritis, pancreatic or pelvic referred pain.</li> </ul> </details> [sc name="jitl-link-03" Q="Back Pain"][/sc]
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">History taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Pain behaviour</strong>: onset, trigger, site, radiation, movement relationship, and what eases or aggravates it.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Cauda equina screen</strong>: saddle numbness, urinary retention, overflow incontinence, reduced perineal sensation, bilateral leg symptoms.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Fracture / cancer / infection red flags</strong>: trauma, steroids, osteoporosis, fever, weight loss, cancer history, IV drug use.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="104.4"][/sc]<strong>Function and psychosocial impact</strong>: mobility, sleep, work, fear-avoidance, mood, and what the patient has stopped doing.</li> <li><strong>Novice prompt:</strong> ask directly whether the pain behaves like a back problem or whether the patient feels “ill” with it.</li> </ul> </details> [sc name="jitl-link-04" Q="Back Pain"][/sc]
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>Observation and movement</strong>: gait, posture, ability to stand, sit, turn, and mobilise.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>Spinal and neurological examination</strong>: tenderness, range of movement, power, reflexes, sensation, and objective weakness.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1273" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>Straight leg raise and abdominal/systemic check</strong>: support sciatica but also look for non-spinal pathology.</li> <li><strong>Teaching rule:</strong> normal neurology makes cauda equina less likely, but it does not rescue a classic bladder or saddle history.</li> </ul> </details> [sc name="jitl-link-05" Q="Back Pain"][/sc]
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1273" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Emergency:</strong> suspected cauda equina syndrome, rupturing AAA, or severe progressive neurology.</li> <li><strong>Same-day urgent review:</strong> suspected fracture, infection, malignancy, or rapidly worsening radiculopathy.</li> <li><strong>Teaching rule:</strong> severe pain without red flags is still often mechanical, but severe pain with bladder or saddle symptoms is not routine sciatica.</li> </ul> </details> [sc name="jitl-link-06" Q="Back Pain"][/sc]
