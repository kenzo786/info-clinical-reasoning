[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="101" title="Overview" color="green" step="1" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.101" role="button">Overview</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Core idea:</strong> acute self-limiting viral URTI with blocked or runny nose, sneezing, sore throat, and mild cough.</li> <li><strong>Task:</strong> rule out influenza, COVID-19, sinusitis, pneumonia, and other complications.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="102" title="Pathophysiology" color="green" step="2" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.102" role="button">Pathophysiology/basic science</summary> <ul> <li>Viral mucosal inflammation drives congestion and mucus.</li> <li>Symptoms usually settle with time and supportive care.</li> </ul></details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Common:</strong> common cold, allergic rhinitis, viral pharyngitis.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Often confused with:</strong> influenza, COVID-19, acute sinusitis.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> pneumonia, meningitis, nasal foreign body.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">Focused history taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Symptom pattern:</strong> gradual onset coryzal illness with mild cough and sore throat.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Exposure/context:</strong> seasonal itch, recurrent episodes, contacts, COVID risk.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Risk factors:</strong> frailty, pregnancy, chronic disease, immunosuppression.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Focused examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>When to examine:</strong> severe illness, uncertainty, red flags, or child who looks unwell.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>Localising signs:</strong> chest, throat, ears, and nose help separate sinusitis or pneumonia.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>General appearance:</strong> dehydration, tachypnoea, or toxicity changes urgency.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Urgent:</strong> breathlessness, chest pain, confusion, stridor, drooling, neck stiffness, reduced consciousness.</li> <li><strong>Prompt review:</strong> high fever, unilateral facial pain, worsening after improvement, prolonged illness.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="107" title="Investigations" color="yellow" step="7" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.107" role="button">Investigations strategy &amp; interpretation</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="107"][/sc]No routine tests for a typical cold.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="107"][/sc]Consider COVID / flu testing when locally relevant.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="107"][/sc]Review for sinusitis or chest infection if symptoms evolve.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="108"][/sc]Antibiotics do not help uncomplicated colds.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="108"][/sc]Supportive care and saline measures are usually enough.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="108"][/sc]Decongestants should be short-term and cautious.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="109" title="Diagnostic Timeout" color="pink" step="9" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.109" role="button">Diagnostic timeout &amp; cognitive biases</summary> <ul> <li>Do not miss allergic rhinitis because the patient says “cold”.</li> <li>Do not miss flu, COVID-19, or sinusitis when fever or facial pain is prominent.</li> <li>Do not use antibiotics for an uncomplicated viral illness.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Offer:</strong> rest, fluids, analgesia, and saline nasal measures.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Consider:</strong> cautious, short-term decongestant advice for suitable adults only.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Return urgently if:</strong> breathing worsens, fever rises, or facial/chest pain develops.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="111" title="Special Populations" color="blue" step="11" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.111" role="button">Special populations and role-aware notes</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Children:</strong> simple supportive care; lower threshold for review if unwell.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Pregnancy / frailty:</strong> keep medicines cautious and review earlier if deteriorating.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1295" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Pharmacy role:</strong> suitable for simple self-limiting illness, not severe or atypical cases.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1295" anchor="112" title="Illustrative Cases" color="grey" step="12" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-grey" open> <summary id="s-is.112" role="button">Illustrative cases</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Typical cold:</strong> 3 days of coryza, sore throat, and mild cough, improving.</li> <li><strong>Not a simple cold:</strong> child with unilateral foul nasal discharge.</li> <li><strong>Needs review:</strong> fever, facial pain, and worsening after a week.</li> </ul> </details>
