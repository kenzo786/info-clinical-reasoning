[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1289" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1289" anchor="108.1"][/sc]Localised non-bullous impetigo can often be managed with topical antiseptic treatment and hygiene measures.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1289" anchor="108.2"][/sc]Topical antibiotics should be used cautiously because resistance is a real concern.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1289" anchor="108.3"][/sc]Bullous or widespread impetigo generally needs oral antibiotics rather than OTC treatment.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1289" anchor="108.4"][/sc]Impetigo is contagious, so advice about reducing spread is part of treatment.</li> </ul> </details> [sc name="jitl-link-08" Q="Impetigo"][/sc]

[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1289" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1289" anchor="110.1"][/sc]<strong>Uncomplicated localised disease:</strong> hygiene advice, keep lesions clean, avoid scratching, and use a topical antiseptic if suitable.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1289" anchor="110.2"][/sc]<strong>Widespread or bullous disease:</strong> arrange GP review for oral treatment rather than persisting with self-care alone.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1289" anchor="110.3"][/sc]<strong>Safety-netting:</strong> seek urgent review if redness spreads, fever develops, the child becomes unwell, or the rash worsens despite treatment.</li> </ul> </details> [sc name="jitl-link-10" Q="Impetigo"][/sc]
