[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="101" title="Overview" color="green" step="1" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.101" role="button">Overview</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Common cause of painless bright red rectal bleeding</strong> but cancer-safe thinking is still required.</li> <li><strong>Main task:</strong> separate classic haemorrhoids from fissure, IBD, cancer, or another anorectal cause.</li> <li><strong>Core stance:</strong> conservative treatment first, but don’t skip red flags.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="102" title="Pathophysiology" color="green" step="2" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.102" role="button">Pathophysiology</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Anal vascular cushions</strong> are normal anatomy.</li> <li><strong>Straining / constipation</strong> makes them engorged and prolapse.</li> <li><strong>Why it matters:</strong> bowel habit advice is the key long-term treatment.</li> </ul></details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Common:</strong> haemorrhoids, fissure, pruritus ani.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Often confused with:</strong> skin tags, constipation-related bleeding, rectal polyps.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> colorectal cancer, IBD, anal cancer, iron deficiency anaemia.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">Focused history taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Bleeding pattern</strong>: bright red, painless, on paper vs mixed with stool.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Pain / prolapse</strong>: mild discomfort fits haemorrhoids; severe pain suggests fissure or thrombosis.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Red flags</strong>: change in bowel habit, weight loss, anaemia, abdominal mass, persistent bleeding.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="104.4"][/sc]<strong>Triggers</strong>: constipation, straining, pregnancy, prolonged sitting, anticoagulants.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Focused examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>Perianal inspection</strong>: fissure, thrombosis, dermatitis, skin tags.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>DRE</strong>: if tolerated, look for masses, tenderness, blood.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>Proctoscopy</strong>: consider if the diagnosis remains uncertain.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Urgent cancer pathway:</strong> age over 50 with rectal bleeding, change in bowel habit, weight loss, anaemia, mass.</li> <li><strong>Same-day review:</strong> heavy bleeding, severe pain, unwell patient, thrombosis with marked symptoms.</li> <li><strong>Routine care only:</strong> classic painless bright red bleeding with constipation/straining and no red flags.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="107" title="Investigations" color="yellow" step="7" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.107" role="button">Investigations and decision points</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>No routine tests</strong> for classic uncomplicated cases.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Bloods</strong> if recurrent or significant bleeding suggests anaemia.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Endoscopy / urgent referral</strong> if red flags or age criteria are met.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="108"][/sc]Fibre and fluids are the foundation.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="108"][/sc]Topical steroids / anaesthetics help short term only.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1298" anchor="108"][/sc]Persistent bleeding needs cancer-safe thinking.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="109" title="Diagnostic Timeout" color="pink" step="9" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.109" role="button">Diagnostic timeout</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Pause:</strong> am I assuming every rectal bleed is haemorrhoids?</li> <li><strong>Check:</strong> is pain dominant, is there bowel habit change, or is the bleeding mixed with stool?</li> <li><strong>Bias risk:</strong> treating symptoms without fixing constipation and straining.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Uncomplicated haemorrhoids:</strong> fibre, fluids, toileting advice, short-term symptom relief.</li> <li><strong>Persistent / recurrent symptoms:</strong> reassess and consider referral.</li> <li><strong>Safety-net:</strong> heavy bleeding, black stool, weight loss, bowel habit change, or worsening pain.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="111" title="Special Populations" color="blue" step="11" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.111" role="button">Special populations and role-aware notes</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Pregnancy:</strong> common, but conservative care remains the mainstay.</li> <li><strong>Older adults:</strong> lower threshold for cancer assessment.</li> <li><strong>Anticoagulated patients:</strong> bleeding matters more and needs review.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1298" anchor="112" title="Cases" color="grey" step="12" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-grey" open> <summary id="s-is.112" role="button">Illustrative cases</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Case 1:</strong> bright red blood on wiping with constipation and no red flags can usually be treated conservatively.</li> <li><strong>Case 2:</strong> bleeding with change in bowel habit needs urgent cancer-pathway review.</li> <li><strong>Case 3:</strong> severe pain with a tender lump suggests fissure or thrombosis.</li> </ul> </details>
