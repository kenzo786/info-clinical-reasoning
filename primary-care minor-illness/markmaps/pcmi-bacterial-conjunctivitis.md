[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1285" anchor="101" title="Overview" color="green" step="1" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.101" role="button">Overview</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Clinical challenge:</strong> recognise a sticky superficial red eye while excluding a sight-threatening painful red eye.</li> <li><strong>Typical pattern:</strong> mucopurulent discharge, lids stuck together, gritty irritation, not true pain.</li> <li><strong>Core stance:</strong> antibiotic decisions come after red-flag exclusion, not before.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1285" anchor="102" title="Pathophysiology" color="green" step="2" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-green" open> <summary id="s-is.102" role="button">Pathophysiology/basic science</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Surface infection:</strong> conjunctival bacterial inflammation drives redness and discharge.</li> <li><strong>Sticky eye:</strong> neutrophil-rich discharge explains crusting and stuck lids.</li> <li><strong>Why it matters:</strong> cornea and deeper structures should remain normal in routine disease.</li> </ul></details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1285" anchor="103" title="Differential Diagnosis" color="purple" step="3" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.103" role="button">Differential diagnosis framework</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="103.1"][/sc]<strong>Common and likely:</strong> bacterial conjunctivitis, viral conjunctivitis, blepharoconjunctivitis.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="103.2"][/sc]<strong>Often confused with it:</strong> allergic conjunctivitis, dry eye, foreign body or abrasion.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="103.3"][/sc]<strong>Must not miss:</strong> microbial keratitis, anterior uveitis, acute angle-closure glaucoma.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1285" anchor="104" title="History" color="yellow" step="4" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.104" role="button">Focused history taking</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="104.1"][/sc]<strong>Symptom profile:</strong> stickiness, discharge type, grittiness, itch, unilateral or bilateral spread.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="104.2"][/sc]<strong>Red-flag screen:</strong> true pain, photophobia, reduced vision, trauma, foreign-body sensation.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="104.3"][/sc]<strong>Contact lens and host factors:</strong> contact lens wear, immunosuppression, age under 2, neonate, recurrence.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1285" anchor="105" title="Examination" color="yellow" step="5" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.105" role="button">Focused examination strategy</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="105.1"][/sc]<strong>Visual acuity and general appearance</strong></li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="105.2"][/sc]<strong>Conjunctiva, discharge, cornea, and pupil</strong></li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="105.3"][/sc]<strong>Contact lens and eyelid review when relevant</strong></li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1285" anchor="106" title="Red Flags" color="pink" step="6" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.106" role="button">Red flag synthesis</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li><strong>Urgent / same-day:</strong> pain, photophobia, reduced vision, corneal opacity, ciliary flush, abnormal pupil, or proptosis.</li> <li><strong>Urgent / same-day:</strong> contact lens wearer with painful red eye, neonate with purulent discharge, or recent trauma / surgery.</li> <li><strong>Prompt review:</strong> recurrent, atypical, or non-resolving symptoms.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1285" anchor="107" title="Investigations" color="yellow" step="7" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-yellow" open> <summary id="s-is.107" role="button">Investigations and decision points</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Usually clinical diagnosis</strong> in uncomplicated cases.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Main decision point:</strong> are red flags or contact lens risk present?</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="107"][/sc]<strong>Consider microbiology or specialist input</strong> for recurrent, severe, neonatal, or resistant disease.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1285" anchor="108" title="Evidence & Guidance" color="purple" step="8" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-purple" open> <summary id="s-is.108" role="button">Evidence and guideline pearls</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="108"][/sc]Many cases resolve without antibiotics.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="108"][/sc]Selective or delayed antibiotics support stewardship in low-risk disease.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="108"][/sc]Contact lens wearers should not be managed as routine conjunctivitis if pain is present.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1285" anchor="109" title="Diagnostic Timeout" color="pink" step="9" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-pink" open> <summary id="s-is.109" role="button">Diagnostic timeout &amp; cognitive biases</summary> <ul> <li><strong>Pause:</strong> have I mistaken a generic red eye for conjunctivitis?</li> <li><strong>Check:</strong> have I explicitly asked about pain, vision, light sensitivity, and contact lenses?</li> <li><strong>Bias risk:</strong> reaching for antibiotics before excluding keratitis.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1285" anchor="110" title="Management" color="blue" step="10" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.110" role="button">Management and safety-netting</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Uncomplicated disease:</strong> hygiene, lid cleansing, separate towels, and realistic expectations.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Medication strategy:</strong> lubricants for comfort and selective antibiotics only when justified.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="110"][/sc]<strong>Urgent return:</strong> pain, photophobia, worsening redness, visual change, or failure to improve.</li> </ul> </details>
[sc name="map-steps-and-links" id="1285" anchor="111" title="Special Populations" color="blue" step="11" info-modal="" pathway="display-none" youtube="display-none"][/sc]<details class="kb-accordion kb-parent d-blue" open> <summary id="s-is.111" role="button">Special populations and role-aware notes</summary> <ul class="no-bullet"> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Children under 2 and neonates:</strong> lower threshold for medical review; neonates are urgent.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Older adults:</strong> think about dry eye and practical ability to use drops.</li> <li>[sc name="link-websheet" id="1285" anchor="111"][/sc]<strong>Role-aware care:</strong> only treat as minor illness if serious red-eye features have been actively excluded.</li> </ul> </details>
