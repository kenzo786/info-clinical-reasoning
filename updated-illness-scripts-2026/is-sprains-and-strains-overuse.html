Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Sprains: Excessive force leads to ligament fibre disruption, causing inflammation, pain, and loss of joint stability.
  • Strains: Overstretching or repetitive microtrauma leads to muscle fibre or tendon damage, with inflammation, local bleeding, and reduced contractile strength.
  • Overuse injuries: Chronic microtrauma due to repetitive motion or stress exceeds the tissue's repair capacity, leading to progressive weakening and inflammation.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Acute phase (1-3 days): Pain, swelling, and limited mobility due to inflammation and tissue damage.
  • Subacute phase (4-14 days): Early tissue repair with gradual reduction in swelling and return of function.
  • Chronic phase (weeks to months): Persistent symptoms may occur in severe injuries or with overuse conditions due to inadequate healing.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Ask about pain: Localised to the affected area, often exacerbated by movement or pressure. Results from inflammation and nerve irritation.
  • Ask about swelling: Due to vascular leakage and inflammatory mediators at the injury site.
  • Ask about bruising: Visible skin discolouration due to bleeding under the skin from damaged blood vessels.
  • Ask about stiffness: Reduced range of motion secondary to pain and swelling limiting joint or muscle movement.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Check tenderness: Localised pain on palpation due to tissue damage and inflammation.
  • Check swelling: Observable oedema in the affected area from fluid accumulation.
  • Check reduced strength: Weakness on testing, indicating muscle or tendon damage.
  • Check instability (sprains): Excessive joint movement due to ligament damage, particularly in the ankle or knee.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Severe swelling and bruising: May indicate a complete tear or associated fracture.
  • Inability to bear weight: Suggests severe injury, such as a complete ligament tear or bony injury.
  • Persistent instability: Indicates chronic ligament insufficiency requiring further intervention.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Minimal swelling or pain: May occur in high-grade sprains with nerve disruption or chronic overuse injuries.
  • Delayed symptoms: Symptoms appearing after repetitive activity may suggest a stress-related overuse condition.
  • How it gets missed: atypical features and comorbidity can mask sprains and strains (including overuse).
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Baseline and bedside: X-ray: To exclude fractures, especially in high-risk injuries like ankle sprains.
  • Targeted and monitoring: MRI: Detailed imaging for soft tissue injuries such as ligament tears or muscle strains; Ultrasound: Useful for tendon injuries and assessing.

Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Fracture - More severe pain, deformity, and inability to bear weight.
  • Tendon rupture - Palpable gap in the tendon, inability to activate the affected muscle.
  • Contusion - Localised bruising with minimal functional impairment.
