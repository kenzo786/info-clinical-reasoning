Inborn Errors of Metabolism
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Inborn errors of metabolism (IEM) are a group of rare genetic disorders caused by defects in specific enzymes or cofactors required for the metabolism of carbohydrates, proteins, fats, or nucleotides.
  • UKMLA essentials: recognise inborn features, identify urgent deterioration, prioritise focused testing.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • [sc name="1095-jitl-epidemiology-link" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Risk factors: Risk factors for inborn errors of metabolism include relevant predisposition, comorbidity, and exposure history.
  • Higher-risk or missed groups: Inborn Errors of Metabolism may be missed when presentation is atypical or obscured by comorbidity.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Defective enzyme/cofactor: Mutations lead to a lack of activity or absent enzymes required for metabolic pathways.
  • Accumulation of toxic substrates: E.g., phenylketonuria (PKU) results in toxic phenylalanine levels affecting the brain.
  • Deficiency of essential products: E.g., glycogen storage diseases result in impaired glucose availability.
  • Energy deficit: Disrupted metabolism impairs cellular energy production, particularly affecting energy-demanding organs like the brain, liver, and heart.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Neonatal phase: Many IEMs present shortly after birth, often triggered by feeding.
  • Infancy and childhood: Gradual symptom onset due to cumulative effects, such as developmental delays or recurrent crises.
  • Adulthood: Some IEMs remain asymptomatic until metabolic stress (e.g., fasting, illness) occurs.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Ask about lethargy or poor feeding: Common in neonates due to metabolic toxicity impairing energy production.
  • Ask about developmental delay: Due to accumulation of toxic substrates affecting brain development.
  • Ask about vomiting: Frequently seen in urea cycle defects or organic acidemias due to ammonia or acid build-up.
  • Ask about seizures: Resulting from energy deficiency or toxic metabolic byproducts.
  • Ask about failure to thrive: Chronic metabolic imbalances impair normal growth and development.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Check hypotonia: Due to impaired energy metabolism in muscles.
  • Check hepatomegaly: Often seen in storage diseases or liver involvement in metabolic disorders.
  • Check metabolic acidosis: Evident in blood gas analysis, caused by organic acid build-up.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Acute encephalopathy: Indicates significant ammonia toxicity or hypoglycaemia; urgent intervention is required to prevent permanent damage.
  • Unexplained metabolic acidosis: Suggests organic acidemias or mitochondrial disorders.
  • Recurrent crises: Episodes of decompensation triggered by stressors suggest metabolic instability.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Adult-onset fatigue or muscle weakness: Can indicate late-onset metabolic myopathy or mitochondrial disorder.
  • Psychiatric symptoms: Such as mood changes in untreated PKU.
  • How it gets missed: atypical features and comorbidity can mask inborn errors of metabolism.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Baseline and bedside: Plasma amino acids: Elevated phenylalanine in PKU.
  • Targeted and monitoring: Urine organic acids: Diagnostic for organic acidemias; Ammonia levels: Elevated in urea cycle disorders.

Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Sepsis - Distinguished by positive cultures and infectious markers.
  • Hypoxic-ischaemic encephalopathy - Usually has a clear perinatal hypoxia history.
  • Congenital heart disease - Cyanosis and abnormal echocardiogram findings.
  • Related alternative diagnosis - Distinguish from inborn errors of metabolism using focused history, examination, and targeted investigations.
Treatment [sc name="ask-ai-112" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Immediate priorities: Assess severity, physiological stability, and immediate complications.
  • Core care: Deliver condition-specific management for inborn errors of metabolism, treat underlying drivers, and coordinate appropriate specialist input.
  • Monitoring and follow-up: Reassess symptom trajectory, examination findings, and objective markers to confirm response in inborn errors of metabolism.
  • Escalation and safety-netting: Escalate urgently for clinical deterioration, evolving red flags, or failure to improve in suspected inborn errors of metabolism.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Inborn Errors of Metabolism"][/sc]
  • Neurodevelopmental delay: From chronic exposure to toxic metabolites.
  • Organ damage: Hepatic or renal damage due to chronic metabolic imbalances.
