Spondyloarthropathies
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Spondyloarthropathies"][/sc]
  • Spondyloarthropathies (SpA) are a group of inflammatory rheumatic diseases that share clinical, genetic, and radiological features.
  • UKMLA essentials: recognise spondyloarthropathies features, identify urgent deterioration, prioritise focused testing.

Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Spondyloarthropathies"][/sc]
  • HLA-B27 misfolding leads to endoplasmic reticulum stress and an aberrant immune response.
  • Inflammatory cytokines such as TNF-α and IL-17 promote synovitis and enthesitis, leading to bone erosion and abnormal bone repair.
  • Chronic inflammation results in ankylosis (fusion of joints), particularly in axial SpA, reducing mobility over time.
  • Enthesitis at peripheral sites contributes to pain and stiffness.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Spondyloarthropathies"][/sc]
  • Initial presentation often includes insidious onset of inflammatory back pain or enthesitis.
  • Progression varies but may include episodic peripheral arthritis or axial stiffness over months to years.
  • Chronic disease can lead to permanent structural damage, such as spinal fusion, in later stages.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Spondyloarthropathies"][/sc]
  • Ask about inflammatory back pain - Insidious onset of pain, worse at night and improving with activity; due to sacroiliac joint and vertebral inflammation.
  • Ask about peripheral arthritis - Asymmetric oligoarthritis affecting large joints, caused by synovial inflammation.
  • Ask about morning stiffness - Lasts >30 minutes; reflects synovitis and enthesitis-driven stiffness.
  • Ask about enthesitis - Pain and tenderness at tendon insertion sites like the Achilles tendon; due to localised inflammation.
  • Ask about fatigue - Related to systemic inflammation and cytokine-mediated effects.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Spondyloarthropathies"][/sc]
  • Check reduced spinal mobility - Loss of lumbar flexion, tested by Schober's test; reflects ankylosis.
  • Check sacroiliac joint tenderness - Detected on palpation or compression tests; due to inflammation.
  • Check peripheral joint swelling - Indicative of arthritis.
  • Check dactylitis - "Sausage digits," caused by diffuse inflammation of a digit.
  • Check psoriatic skin lesions - Seen in psoriatic arthritis; result from hyperproliferative keratinocytes and inflammation.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Spondyloarthropathies"][/sc]
  • Sudden severe back pain - Could indicate a vertebral fracture, especially in advanced disease with osteoporosis.
  • Neurological deficits - Suggest cauda equina syndrome from spinal stenosis or nerve compression.
  • Systemic symptoms - Fever or weight loss may signify malignancy or systemic infection mimicking SpA.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Spondyloarthropathies"][/sc]
  • Isolated enthesitis - Without axial or peripheral symptoms; may be mistaken for mechanical injury.
  • Unilateral sacroiliitis - Inflammatory back pain confined to one side, delaying diagnosis.
  • Extra-articular features - Uveitis, inflammatory bowel disease, or psoriasis as the presenting symptom.
  • How it gets missed: atypical features and comorbidity can mask spondyloarthropathies.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Spondyloarthropathies"][/sc]
  • Baseline and bedside: Imaging - X-rays show sacroiliitis (sclerosis, erosion), while MRI detects early bone marrow oedema.
  • Targeted and monitoring: Targeted investigations to confirm diagnosis, assess complications, and monitor response in spondyloarthropathies.

Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Spondyloarthropathies"][/sc]
  • Mechanical back pain - Lacks inflammatory features and morning stiffness.
  • Rheumatoid arthritis - Predominantly affects small joints symmetrically, unlike SpA.
  • Fibromyalgia - Pain is widespread, lacks inflammatory or imaging findings.
  • Related alternative diagnosis - Distinguish from spondyloarthropathies using focused history, examination, and targeted investigations.

Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Spondyloarthropathies"][/sc]
  • Spinal fusion - Leading to reduced mobility and disability.
  • Fractures - Osteoporotic fractures due to chronic inflammation and immobility.
  • Cardiovascular disease - Increased risk from chronic systemic inflammation.
