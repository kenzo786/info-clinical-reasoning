Functional Disorder
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • A functional disorder refers to a medical condition characterised by symptoms that cannot be explained by structural, biochemical, or pathological abnormalities.
  • UKMLA essentials: recognise functional features, identify urgent deterioration, prioritise focused testing.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • [sc name="1095-jitl-epidemiology-link" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • Risk factors: Risk factors for functional disorder include relevant predisposition, comorbidity, and exposure history.
  • Higher-risk or missed groups: Functional Disorder may be missed when presentation is atypical or obscured by comorbidity.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • Central Sensitisation: Hyperexcitability of neurons in the central nervous system leads to amplification of pain or other sensory signals.
  • Dysregulated Stress Response: Abnormalities in the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis increase susceptibility to symptoms under stress.
  • Gut-Brain Axis Dysregulation: Altered communication between the gastrointestinal system and central nervous system contributes to symptoms such as pain, bloating, or altered bowel habits.
  • Neurotransmitter Imbalance: Alterations in serotonin, norepinephrine, and dopamine pathways are implicated in symptom modulation.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • Acute Phase: Symptoms may develop suddenly, often following a trigger such as stress, infection, or trauma.
  • Chronic Phase: Symptoms persist for months to years, typically fluctuating in severity. Relapses are common, often triggered by stress or lifestyle changes.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • Ask about chronic Fatigue: Persistent exhaustion, unrelated to exertion. Likely due to central nervous system dysregulation.
  • Ask about diffuse Pain: Generalised musculoskeletal pain, often linked to central sensitisation.
  • Ask about gastrointestinal Symptoms: Includes bloating, diarrhoea, or constipation, associated with gut-brain axis dysfunction.
  • Ask about non-Specific Neurological Symptoms: Headaches, dizziness, or paraesthesia, attributed to altered central processing of sensory inputs.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • Check normal Physical Examination: Despite severe symptoms, physical and neurological examinations are typically unremarkable.
  • Check positive Tender Points: Found in some conditions like fibromyalgia, indicating heightened pain sensitivity.
  • Check signs of Hypervigilance: Anxiety, tachycardia, or hyperventilation, reflecting autonomic dysregulation.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • Unexplained Weight Loss: May indicate malignancy or systemic disease.
  • Neurological Deficits: Weakness or sensory loss necessitates investigation for organic pathology.
  • Haemoptysis or Rectal Bleeding: Red flags for cancer or severe gastrointestinal disease.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • Minimal Symptoms: Subtle fatigue or mild gastrointestinal complaints may mask the disorder.
  • Overlap with Psychiatric Symptoms: Prominent anxiety or depression may obscure the functional diagnosis.
  • How it gets missed: atypical features and comorbidity can mask functional disorder.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • Baseline and bedside: Exclusion Tests: Blood tests, imaging, and endoscopy to rule out organic causes. Normal findings are typical.
  • Targeted and monitoring: Functional Assessment: Tests like the Functional Bowel Disorder Severity Index to assess symptom impact.
Problem Representation [sc name="ask-ai-110" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • A 35-year-old woman presents with a 2-year history of widespread musculoskeletal pain, fatigue, and intermittent bloating, exacerbated by stress. Investigations, including blood tests and imaging, are unremarkable.
  • Patient with a 2-year history of widespread musculoskeletal pain, fatigue, and intermittent bloating, exacerbated by stress. Investigations, including blood tests and imaging, are unremarkable, consistent with functional disorder.
Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • Chronic Fatigue Syndrome - Differs by predominant fatigue without widespread pain.
  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome - Overlaps but focuses on gastrointestinal symptoms.
  • Autoimmune Disorders - Positive serological markers and inflammatory signs in tests differentiate them.
  • Related alternative diagnosis - Distinguish from functional disorder using focused history, examination, and targeted investigations.
Treatment [sc name="ask-ai-112" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • Immediate priorities: Assess severity, physiological stability, and immediate complications.
  • Core care: Deliver condition-specific management for functional disorder, treat underlying drivers, and coordinate appropriate specialist input.
  • Monitoring and follow-up: Reassess symptom trajectory, examination findings, and objective markers to confirm response in functional disorder.
  • Escalation and safety-netting: Escalate urgently for clinical deterioration, evolving red flags, or failure to improve in suspected functional disorder.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Functional Disorder"][/sc]
  • Chronic Disability: Long-term functional impairment due to persistent symptoms.
  • Psychiatric Comorbidities: High prevalence of depression and anxiety.
  • Healthcare Overutilisation: Frequent, unnecessary investigations and referrals.
