Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)"][/sc]
  • Juvenile idiopathic arthritis (JIA) is a heterogeneous group of autoimmune and autoinflammatory diseases characterised by arthritis of unknown cause lasting at least six weeks, with onset before the age, with clinically relevant features.
  • UKMLA essentials: recognise juvenile features, identify urgent deterioration, prioritise focused testing.

Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)"][/sc]
  • Genetic susceptibility, including HLA associations, predisposes to an abnormal immune response.
  • Environmental triggers, such as infections, can initiate immune dysregulation.
  • Inappropriate activation of T-cells and cytokines (e.g., TNF-α, IL-1, IL-6) leads to synovial inflammation.
  • Chronic inflammation results in synovial hyperplasia, angiogenesis, and joint damage due to pannus formation.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)"][/sc]
  • Initial presentation often includes joint pain, stiffness, and swelling over weeks to months.
  • Subtypes of JIA have variable courses:
    • Oligoarticular: Flares with remission possible.
    • Polyarticular: Chronic with persistent inflammation.
    • Systemic: Waxing and waning fevers with inflammatory episodes.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)"][/sc]
  • Ask about joint Pain - Caused by synovial inflammation, leading to pressure and sensitisation of pain fibres.
  • Ask about morning Stiffness - Due to accumulation of inflammatory mediators overnight.
  • Ask about joint Swelling - Results from synovial hyperplasia and effusion.
  • Ask about fatigue - Likely secondary to systemic inflammation and cytokine dysregulation.
  • Ask about fever (Systemic JIA) - Driven by IL-6 and other pro-inflammatory cytokines.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)"][/sc]
  • Check warmth over Joints - Indicates active inflammation and increased vascular flow.
  • Check limited Range of Motion - Due to pain, effusion, or synovial thickening.
  • Check growth Abnormalities - Overgrowth or undergrowth caused by localised inflammation or corticosteroid use.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)"][/sc]
  • Uveitis - Chronic inflammation of the eye; risk of vision loss if untreated.
  • Systemic Features - High fevers, hepatosplenomegaly, or serositis may indicate systemic JIA.
  • Failure to Thrive - Reflects chronic disease or systemic involvement.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)"][/sc]
  • Arthritis limited to small joints, which may mimic early rheumatoid arthritis.
  • Recurrent fevers without clear joint involvement, suggesting systemic onset.
  • How it gets missed: atypical features and comorbidity can mask juvenile idiopathic arthritis (jia).
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)"][/sc]
  • Baseline and bedside: ESR/CRP - Elevated due to systemic inflammation; Rheumatoid Factor (RF) - Positive in a subset of polyarticular JIA.
  • Targeted and monitoring: ANA - Frequently positive in oligoarticular JIA and associated with uveitis risk; Joint Imaging (Ultrasound/MRI) - Detects synovitis, effusion, and damage.

Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)"][/sc]
  • Rheumatic Fever - Differentiated by migratory arthritis and carditis.
  • Septic Arthritis - Acute onset with fever and single joint involvement.
  • Leukaemia - Associated with systemic symptoms like pallor, bruising, and hepatosplenomegaly.
  • Related alternative diagnosis - Distinguish from juvenile idiopathic arthritis (jia) using focused history, examination, and targeted investigations.

Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)"][/sc]
  • Joint Destruction - From chronic uncontrolled inflammation.
  • Growth Retardation - Due to chronic disease or corticosteroid use.
  • Uveitis - Risk of blindness if untreated.
