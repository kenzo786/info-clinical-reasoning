Pancreatic Insufficiency
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Pancreatic Insufficiency"][/sc]
  • Pancreatic insufficiency is the inability of the exocrine pancreas to produce and secrete adequate amounts of digestive enzymes, leading to malabsorption of nutrients.
  • UKMLA essentials: recognise pancreatic features, identify urgent deterioration, prioritise focused testing.

Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Pancreatic Insufficiency"][/sc]
  • Damage to the pancreatic acinar cells, due to chronic inflammation (e.g., chronic pancreatitis), fibrosis, or surgical resection, leads to a reduction in the production of digestive enzymes, particularly lipase, amylase, and proteases. This enzymatic deficiency causes impaired digestion and absorption of fats, proteins, and carbohydrates, resulting in steatorrhoea, weight loss, and malnutrition. Fat digestion is particularly impaired because lipase has a shorter half-life than other enzymes and requires bile acids for optimal activity.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Pancreatic Insufficiency"][/sc]
  • Pancreatic insufficiency often develops gradually over time, particularly in chronic conditions like chronic pancreatitis or cystic fibrosis. Symptoms can be insidious at first, with fat malabsorption occurring before protein and carbohydrate malabsorption. In cases of acute pancreatic damage (e.g., post-pancreatic surgery), symptoms may develop more rapidly.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Pancreatic Insufficiency"][/sc]
  • Ask about steatorrhoea (fatty stools) - Due to the deficiency of pancreatic lipase, undigested fats are excreted in the stool, causing bulky, oily, pale, and malodorous.
  • Ask about weight loss - Malabsorption of fats and proteins leads to calorie deficiency and subsequent weight loss.
  • Ask about diarrhoea - Incomplete digestion of macronutrients can lead to osmotic diarrhoea as unabsorbed nutrients draw water into the intestines.
  • Ask about abdominal pain or discomfort - Common in chronic pancreatitis-related insufficiency due to underlying inflammation and fibrosis of the pancreas.
  • Ask about flatulence and bloating - Malabsorbed carbohydrates and fats undergo fermentation by gut bacteria, producing gas and causing bloating.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Pancreatic Insufficiency"][/sc]
  • Check cachexia - Progressive weight loss and muscle wasting due to severe malnutrition.
  • Check signs of malnutrition - Dry skin, hair loss, and muscle wasting may be seen due to inadequate absorption of fats, proteins, and vitamins.
  • Check abdominal tenderness - Particularly in patients with underlying chronic pancreatitis, there may be tenderness in the epigastric region.
  • Check oedema - In severe cases, low albumin from protein malabsorption may cause peripheral oedema.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Pancreatic Insufficiency"][/sc]
  • Unexplained weight loss - This suggests significant malabsorption and may indicate advancing pancreatic insufficiency or an underlying malignancy.
  • New onset diabetes - Can signify loss of endocrine pancreatic function in addition to exocrine insufficiency, especially in chronic pancreatitis.
  • Jaundice - Suggests possible pancreatic duct obstruction, which may be caused by pancreatic cancer.
  • Severe, persistent abdominal pain - May indicate complications such as pancreatic pseudocyst or malignancy.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Pancreatic Insufficiency"][/sc]
  • Mild or intermittent symptoms - In early or mild pancreatic insufficiency, symptoms like steatorrhoea or weight loss may be intermittent or subtle, leading to delayed diagnosis.
  • Non-gastrointestinal presentations - Fat-soluble vitamin deficiencies (e.g., Vitamin D deficiency leading to osteoporosis) may be the first clue to pancreatic insufficiency, especially in older patients.
  • How it gets missed: atypical features and comorbidity can mask pancreatic insufficiency.

Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Pancreatic Insufficiency"][/sc]
  • Coeliac disease - While both conditions cause malabsorption, coeliac disease is associated with gluten sensitivity and will.
  • Inflammatory bowel disease - Presents with chronic diarrhoea and weight loss but is typically associated with inflammatory markers.
  • Bile acid malabsorption - Causes steatorrhoea but without pancreatic enzyme deficiencies; SeHCAT scan can confirm bile acid loss.
  • Small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (SIBO) - Distinguish from pancreatic insufficiency using focused history, examination, and targeted investigations.

  • Escalation and safety-netting: Escalate urgently for clinical deterioration, evolving red flags, or failure to improve in suspected pancreatic insufficiency.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Pancreatic Insufficiency"][/sc]
  • Malnutrition - Chronic malabsorption leads to significant nutrient deficiencies, muscle wasting, and weight loss.
  • Osteoporosis - Caused by Vitamin D deficiency and malabsorption of calcium.
  • Diabetes mellitus - In advanced cases, destruction of both exocrine and endocrine tissue leads to insulin deficiency and diabetes.
  • Pancreatic cancer - Long-standing chronic pancreatitis is a risk factor for developing pancreatic carcinoma.
