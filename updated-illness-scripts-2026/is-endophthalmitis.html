Endophthalmitis
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Endophthalmitis"][/sc]
  • Endophthalmitis is a severe intraocular inflammatory condition involving the aqueous humour or vitreous humour, commonly caused by infection (bacterial or fungal) or, less frequently, by non-infectious inflammatory processes.
  • UKMLA essentials: recognise endophthalmitis features, identify urgent deterioration, prioritise focused testing.

  • [sc name="1095-jitl-epidemiology-link" Q="Endophthalmitis"][/sc]
  • Risk factors: Risk factors for endophthalmitis include relevant predisposition, comorbidity, and exposure history.
  • Higher-risk or missed groups: Endophthalmitis may be missed when presentation is atypical or obscured by comorbidity.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Endophthalmitis"][/sc]
  • Pathogens (bacteria or fungi) gain entry to the eye via surgical procedures, trauma, or endogenous spread.
  • The infection leads to rapid replication of microorganisms in the intraocular space.
  • This triggers a severe inflammatory response with release of cytokines, leukocyte infiltration, and increased vascular permeability, causing tissue damage and impaired vision.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Endophthalmitis"][/sc]
  • Acute Endophthalmitis: Symptoms develop within hours to days after exposure (e.g., post-surgery or trauma).
  • Chronic Endophthalmitis: Symptoms evolve slowly over weeks to months, often due to less virulent organisms such as Propionibacterium acnes.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Endophthalmitis"][/sc]
  • Ask about severe eye pain - Due to inflammatory mediators and elevated intraocular pressure.
  • Ask about decreased vision - Caused by vitreous opacities and inflammation obscuring the visual axis.
  • Ask about photophobia - Resulting from ciliary body inflammation.
  • Ask about redness - Conjunctival injection due to vascular response to inflammation.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Endophthalmitis"][/sc]
  • Check hypopyon - Accumulation of inflammatory cells in the anterior chamber due to severe inflammation.
  • Check corneal oedema - Secondary to endothelial dysfunction caused by inflammation.
  • Check vitreous haze - Indicative of inflammation in the vitreous body.
  • Check loss of red reflex - Caused by vitreous opacities obstructing the passage of light to the retina.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Endophthalmitis"][/sc]
  • Rapid vision loss - May signify aggressive infection threatening permanent damage.
  • Hypopyon - Suggests severe intraocular inflammation and risk of complications like retinal detachment.
  • Severe pain unresponsive to standard measures - Indicates significant intraocular pressure or uncontrolled infection.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Endophthalmitis"][/sc]
  • Chronic, low-grade symptoms with intermittent blurred vision and minimal pain, often due to low-virulence pathogens.
  • How it gets missed: atypical features and comorbidity can mask endophthalmitis.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Endophthalmitis"][/sc]
  • Baseline and bedside: Aqueous or vitreous sample culture - To identify causative microorganisms.
  • Targeted and monitoring: Ocular ultrasound - Shows vitreous opacities and possible retinal detachment; Slit-lamp examination - Reveals hypopyon, anterior chamber reaction, and keratic precipitates.

Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Endophthalmitis"][/sc]
  • Uveitis - Lacks hypopyon and typically less pain.
  • Acute angle-closure glaucoma - Often includes systemic symptoms like nausea and has elevated intraocular pressure.
  • Keratitis - Localised corneal infiltrates rather than intraocular inflammation.
  • Related alternative diagnosis - Distinguish from endophthalmitis using focused history, examination, and targeted investigations.

  • Immediate priorities: Assess severity, physiological stability, and immediate complications.
  • Core care: Deliver condition-specific management for endophthalmitis, treat underlying drivers, and coordinate appropriate specialist input.
  • Monitoring and follow-up: Reassess symptom trajectory, examination findings, and objective markers to confirm response in endophthalmitis.
  • Escalation and safety-netting: Escalate urgently for clinical deterioration, evolving red flags, or failure to improve in suspected endophthalmitis.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Endophthalmitis"][/sc]
  • Retinal detachment - Due to inflammatory and infectious damage to retinal layers.
  • Permanent vision loss - From structural damage or optic nerve involvement.
  • Phthisis bulbi - Atrophy of the eyeball following severe uncontrolled infection.
