Mechanical Neck Pain
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Mechanical Neck Pain"][/sc]
  • Mechanical neck pain is a musculoskeletal condition characterised by localised pain in the cervical spine, often exacerbated by movement, posture, or prolonged static positions.
  • UKMLA essentials: recognise mechanical features, identify urgent deterioration, prioritise focused testing.

  • Higher-risk or missed groups: Mechanical Neck Pain may be missed when presentation is atypical or obscured by comorbidity.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Mechanical Neck Pain"][/sc]
  • Caused by strain, overuse, or minor trauma to cervical spine structures such as muscles, ligaments, intervertebral discs, and facet joints.
  • Microtrauma or poor posture can lead to local inflammation, muscle spasm, and pain receptor sensitisation in the cervical spine.
  • Altered biomechanics may further perpetuate the cycle of pain and stiffness.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Mechanical Neck Pain"][/sc]
  • Acute phase: Sudden onset, often after identifiable strain, lasting days to weeks.
  • Subacute phase: Gradual improvement over weeks to months, though symptoms may persist.
  • Chronic phase: Symptoms lasting >3 months, often linked to poor ergonomics or recurrent episodes.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Mechanical Neck Pain"][/sc]
  • Ask about localised neck pain - Commonly worsens with movement or maintaining one position for prolonged periods, due to mechanical irritation and inflammation of cervical structures.
  • Ask about stiffness - Frequently noted in the morning or after prolonged inactivity, caused by muscle spasm and joint restriction.
  • Ask about reduced range of motion - A consequence of pain, stiffness, and muscle guarding.
  • Ask about non-specific headache - Referred pain from cervical structures, often occipital or suboccipital in location.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Mechanical Neck Pain"][/sc]
  • Check tenderness over cervical spine - Indicative of local inflammation or muscle strain.
  • Check restricted cervical spine movements - Pain and spasm limit flexion, extension, or rotation.
  • Check postural abnormalities - Forward head posture or asymmetry due to muscle imbalance.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Mechanical Neck Pain"][/sc]
  • Severe or progressive neurological symptoms - May indicate cervical radiculopathy or myelopathy.
  • Unrelenting night pain or systemic symptoms - Raises concern for malignancy or infection.
  • History of significant trauma - Suggests possible fracture or dislocation.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Mechanical Neck Pain"][/sc]
  • Chronic low-grade pain with mild neurological symptoms, which may mimic degenerative disc disease or fibromyalgia.
  • Bilateral symptoms with gait instability, suggesting spinal cord involvement rather than localised mechanical pain.
  • How it gets missed: atypical features and comorbidity can mask mechanical neck pain.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Mechanical Neck Pain"][/sc]
  • Baseline and bedside: X-ray - May show normal findings or age-related degenerative changes, used to rule out significant trauma.
  • Targeted and monitoring: Targeted investigations to confirm diagnosis, assess complications, and monitor response in mechanical neck pain.

Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Mechanical Neck Pain"][/sc]
  • Cervical radiculopathy - Distinguished by radiating arm pain and neurological deficits.
  • Cervical myelopathy - Presents with bilateral neurological symptoms and gait disturbance.
  • Fibromyalgia - Generalised pain and tenderness without localised signs.
  • Ankylosing spondylitis - Chronic inflammatory back pain with systemic symptoms.

  • Escalation and safety-netting: Escalate urgently for clinical deterioration, evolving red flags, or failure to improve in suspected mechanical neck pain.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Mechanical Neck Pain"][/sc]
  • Chronic pain and disability, linked to poor ergonomics and lack of treatment adherence.
  • Progression to secondary conditions such as cervicogenic headache or radiculopathy in severe cases.
