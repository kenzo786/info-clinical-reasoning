Urethral Stricture
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Urethral Stricture"][/sc]
  • Urethral stricture is a condition characterised by the narrowing of the urethral lumen due to scarring or fibrosis.
  • UKMLA essentials: recognise urethral features, identify urgent deterioration, prioritise focused testing.

Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Urethral Stricture"][/sc]
  • Initial insult (e.g., trauma, infection) leads to inflammation and disruption of the urothelium and underlying tissue.
  • Healing response involves fibroblast proliferation and excessive collagen deposition, resulting in scar formation.
  • Fibrosis causes concentric narrowing of the urethral lumen, obstructing urinary flow.
  • Chronic obstruction can lead to secondary complications such as bladder wall thickening, diverticula, and upper urinary tract damage.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Urethral Stricture"][/sc]
  • Acute phase: Onset of symptoms after trauma or infection, marked by inflammation and initial narrowing.
  • Chronic phase: Gradual worsening of obstruction over weeks to months due to progressive fibrosis.
  • Stable phase: Long-term complications such as recurrent urinary tract infections or bladder dysfunction may develop if untreated.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Urethral Stricture"][/sc]
  • Ask about decreased urinary flow - Results from mechanical obstruction of the urethral lumen by fibrotic tissue.
  • Ask about straining during urination - Occurs due to increased intravesical pressure required to overcome the obstruction.
  • Ask about incomplete bladder emptying - Due to the obstruction, residual urine remains in the bladder.
  • Ask about dysuria - Inflammatory changes or infection associated with the stricture can irritate the urethra.
  • Ask about recurrent urinary tract infections (UTIs) - Stasis of urine promotes bacterial growth.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Urethral Stricture"][/sc]
  • Check palpable bladder - Indicative of urinary retention due to obstructed outflow.
  • Check dribbling of urine - Reflects incomplete bladder emptying and overflow incontinence.
  • Check urethral discharge - Seen in cases where infection coexists, particularly with a history of urethritis.
  • Check decreased urinary stream calibre - Often observed directly during urination.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Urethral Stricture"][/sc]
  • Acute urinary retention - Complete inability to void, necessitating emergency intervention to prevent renal damage.
  • Haematuria with clots - May signify severe irritation or associated malignancy.
  • Recurrent UTIs with fever - Could indicate progression to upper tract infection or urosepsis.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Urethral Stricture"][/sc]
  • Asymptomatic presentation: Detected incidentally during investigations for recurrent UTIs or infertility.
  • Chronic pelvic pain: May occur due to prolonged urinary stasis and bladder overactivity.
  • How it gets missed: atypical features and comorbidity can mask urethral stricture.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Urethral Stricture"][/sc]
  • Baseline and bedside: Focused bedside assessment and first-line testing to assess severity and immediate risk in suspected urethral stricture.
  • Targeted and monitoring: Cystoscopy - Direct visualisation of the stricture; Urine culture - To identify secondary infections.

Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Urethral Stricture"][/sc]
  • Benign prostatic hyperplasia - Distinguish from urethral stricture using focused history, examination, and targeted investigations.
  • Bladder neck contracture - Post-surgical scarring at the bladder outlet causing similar obstructive symptoms.
  • Urethral carcinoma - May present with haematuria and a palpable mass.
  • Neurogenic bladder - Differentiated by history of neurological disease and lack of mechanical obstruction.

Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Urethral Stricture"][/sc]
  • Recurrent UTIs - Due to urine stasis.
  • Bladder stones - Resulting from chronic urinary stasis and infection.
  • Hydronephrosis - Due to prolonged obstruction, leading to renal impairment if untreated.
