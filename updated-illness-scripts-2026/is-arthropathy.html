Arthropathy
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Arthropathy refers to a group of conditions characterised by joint pain, inflammation, or degeneration.
  • UKMLA essentials: recognise arthropathy features, identify urgent deterioration, prioritise focused testing.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • [sc name="1095-jitl-epidemiology-link" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Risk factors: Risk of arthropathy rises with relevant predisposition, comorbidity, and exposure profile.
  • Higher-risk or missed groups: Arthropathy may be missed when presentations are atypical or comorbidity obscures key features.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Osteoarthritis: Degeneration of articular cartilage due to mechanical wear and biochemical changes, leading to joint space narrowing, osteophyte formation, and subchondral bone sclerosis.
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis: Autoimmune reaction targeting synovial membranes, causing chronic synovitis, pannus formation, and eventual cartilage and bone erosion.
  • Gout: Hyperuricaemia leads to urate crystal deposition in joints, inducing intense inflammatory response via neutrophil activation.
  • Psoriatic Arthritis: Inflammatory autoimmune disorder associated with psoriasis, involving enthesitis and joint erosion through TNF and IL-17 mediated inflammation.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Acute Phase: Sudden onset in gout and some cases of psoriatic arthritis.
  • Chronic Phase: Progressive in rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, and psoriatic arthritis, with intermittent flares in inflammatory types.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Ask about joint Pain: Most common symptom, worsening with activity in osteoarthritis, while inflammatory types often have pain at rest due to cytokine-induced inflammation.
  • Ask about stiffness: Morning stiffness common in rheumatoid arthritis (lasting >1 hour) due to inflammatory cytokine activity, while shorter in osteoarthritis.
  • Ask about swelling: Inflammatory arthropathies (e.g., rheumatoid arthritis, gout) cause synovial inflammation and joint effusion, leading to swelling.
  • Ask about reduced Range of Motion: Due to cartilage loss and joint degeneration in osteoarthritis or inflammation in rheumatoid arthritis.
  • Ask about fatigue: Often seen in autoimmune arthropathies due to systemic inflammatory mediators.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Check tenderness: Common in inflamed joints, especially in rheumatoid arthritis, due to synovitis.
  • Check joint Deformity: Characteristic in advanced rheumatoid arthritis (e.g., swan-neck deformity) due to chronic joint erosion and ligament involvement.
  • Check crepitus: Grating sensation common in osteoarthritis due to cartilage degeneration.
  • Check effusion: Joint effusion often seen in acute flares of gout and psoriatic arthritis.
  • Check warmth and Redness: In inflammatory conditions (e.g., gout), due to increased blood flow and inflammatory mediators.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Severe and Rapidly Progressive Pain: Indicates septic arthritis, requiring urgent evaluation and treatment to prevent joint destruction.
  • Systemic Symptoms (e.g., Fever, Weight Loss): Suggests potential infection or malignancy-associated arthritis, which needs prompt investigation.
  • Neurological Deficits: Indicates possible spinal cord compression in severe cervical rheumatoid arthritis, a serious complication.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Asymptomatic Hyperuricaemia: Common in gout without joint involvement.
  • Non-Joint Musculoskeletal Pain: Psoriatic arthritis may present with enthesitis without visible joint inflammation initially.
  • How it gets missed: atypical features or comorbidity can mask the underlying diagnosis.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Baseline and bedside: Blood Tests: Elevated inflammatory markers (CRP, ESR) in rheumatoid arthritis; serum uric acid elevated in gout.
  • Targeted and monitoring: Joint Aspiration: Identifies crystals (urate in gout, calcium pyrophosphate in pseudogout) or infectious agents in septic arthritis; Radiographs: Osteophytes and joint.
Problem Representation [sc name="ask-ai-110" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • A 56-year-old female with chronic bilateral knee pain worsening over years, morning stiffness lasting <30 minutes, and restricted range of motion on examination, suggestive of osteoarthritis.
  • Adult with symptom pattern and objective findings suggestive of arthropathy, requiring focused confirmation and risk stratification.
Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Osteoarthritis - Typically non-inflammatory, localised to load-bearing joints, minimal morning stiffness.
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis - Symmetrical polyarthritis with prolonged morning stiffness and systemic symptoms.
  • Gout - Acute monoarthritis, commonly affecting the first metatarsophalangeal joint, with hyperuricaemia.
  • Psoriatic Arthritis - Associated with psoriasis, involves distal interphalangeal joints and enthesitis.
  • Septic Arthritis - Rapid onset with fever, joint effusion, and erythema, typically affecting one joint.
Treatment [sc name="ask-ai-112" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Immediate priorities: Assess physiological stability and urgent complications at presentation, and initiate early stabilisation where required.
  • Core care: Deliver condition-specific treatment for arthropathy, address underlying drivers, and involve appropriate specialist support.
  • Monitoring and follow-up: Reassess symptom trajectory, objective markers, and treatment response over time to detect complications early.
  • Escalation and safety-netting: Worsening symptoms, physiological deterioration, or failure to improve should trigger urgent reassessment for severe or alternative pathology in arthropathy.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Joint Deformity: In rheumatoid arthritis due to chronic inflammation.
  • Joint Destruction: Possible in untreated septic arthritis.
  • Chronic Pain and Disability: Common in advanced osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis, affecting quality of life.
