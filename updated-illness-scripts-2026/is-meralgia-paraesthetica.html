Meralgia Paraesthetica
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Meralgia Paraesthetica"][/sc]
  • Meralgia paraesthetica is a neurological condition caused by compression or irritation of the lateral femoral cutaneous nerve (LFCN), resulting in sensory disturbances such as pain, paraesthesia, or numbness in, with clinically relevant features.
  • UKMLA essentials: recognise meralgia features, identify urgent deterioration, prioritise focused testing.

Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Meralgia Paraesthetica"][/sc]
  • The lateral femoral cutaneous nerve (LFCN) arises from the L2-L3 nerve roots and passes under the inguinal ligament near the anterior superior iliac spine (ASIS).
  • Compression or entrapment of the LFCN at this location leads to impaired nerve function, causing sensory disturbances in its distribution.
  • Contributing factors include mechanical pressure (tight clothing, belts, or pregnancy), trauma, or inflammation of surrounding structures.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Meralgia Paraesthetica"][/sc]
  • Acute onset: Often associated with a specific triggering event such as trauma, surgery, or sudden weight gain.
  • Chronic progression: Symptoms may persist or wax and wane, influenced by ongoing compression or aggravating factors.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Meralgia Paraesthetica"][/sc]
  • Ask about pain - Burning or aching pain in the anterolateral thigh due to sensory nerve irritation.
  • Ask about paraesthesia - Tingling or "pins and needles" sensation caused by partial nerve dysfunction.
  • Ask about numbness - Loss of sensation in the affected area due to significant nerve compression.
  • Ask about exacerbation with standing or walking - Aggravated by activities that increase pressure on the LFCN.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Meralgia Paraesthetica"][/sc]
  • Check hypoaesthesia - Reduced sensation in the LFCN distribution, indicating sensory impairment.
  • Check tenderness over ASIS - Localised tenderness at the site of LFCN entrapment.
  • Check no motor deficits - Normal motor strength in the lower limbs confirms the sensory-only nature of the condition.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Meralgia Paraesthetica"][/sc]
  • Severe, progressive pain - May suggest a more severe compressive neuropathy or secondary causes like tumour.
  • Weakness - Indicates possible involvement of other nerves or conditions like lumbar radiculopathy.
  • Systemic symptoms (fever, weight loss) - Raises concern for malignancy, infection, or systemic disease.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Meralgia Paraesthetica"][/sc]
  • Bilateral symptoms - May occur in cases with systemic causes such as diabetes mellitus.
  • Non-localised pain - Misdiagnosis as musculoskeletal pain if sensory distribution is unclear.
  • How it gets missed: atypical features and comorbidity can mask meralgia paraesthetica.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Meralgia Paraesthetica"][/sc]
  • Baseline and bedside: Clinical diagnosis - Based on characteristic symptoms and exclusion of other causes.
  • Targeted and monitoring: Nerve conduction studies (NCS) - May confirm reduced conduction velocity in the LFCN; Ultrasound or MRI - Useful to rule out.

Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Meralgia Paraesthetica"][/sc]
  • Lumbar radiculopathy - Associated with motor deficits and radicular pain extending below the knee.
  • Diabetic neuropathy - Typically bilateral and affects multiple nerve distributions.
  • Hip osteoarthritis - Associated with joint pain and reduced range of motion.
  • Related alternative diagnosis - Distinguish from meralgia paraesthetica using focused history, examination, and targeted investigations.
