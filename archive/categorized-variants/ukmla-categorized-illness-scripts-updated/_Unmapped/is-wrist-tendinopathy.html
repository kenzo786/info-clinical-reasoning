Wrist Tendinopathy
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Wrist tendinopathy refers to a spectrum of tendon disorders in the wrist, characterised by pain, swelling, and functional impairment. It often involves the tendons of the extensor or flexor compartments and results from overuse, repetitive strain, or acute injury leading to microscopic degeneration rather than inflammation.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Prevalence in the UK GP population: Common, particularly among individuals engaged in repetitive wrist activities or manual labour.
  • Hospital prevalence: Frequently seen in musculoskeletal outpatient settings.
  • Worldwide prevalence: Highly variable but more common in occupational settings requiring repetitive wrist movements.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Repetitive strain or acute overload causes microtears in the tendon fibres.
  • Failure of adequate repair leads to degeneration and disorganisation of collagen fibres (tendinosis).
  • Neurovascular changes and increased sensory nerve endings contribute to chronic pain.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Acute phase: Onset of pain following injury or repetitive use.
  • Subacute phase: Persistent symptoms with activity limitations.
  • Chronic phase: Longstanding pain and functional deficits due to poor healing or recurrent injury.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Pain - Localised pain over the affected tendon, worsened by movement or palpation, due to microscopic tendon damage and nerve sensitisation.
  • Swelling - Swelling over the wrist due to extracellular matrix disruption and increased vascular permeability.
  • Weakness - Reduced grip strength from pain inhibition and impaired tendon function.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Tenderness - Localised tenderness over the affected tendon due to inflammation or degenerative changes.
  • Crepitus - Palpable or audible crepitus with movement, caused by tendon thickening or adhesions.
  • Reduced range of motion - Limitation in active or passive wrist movement, reflecting pain or structural changes.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Severe pain at rest - May indicate tendon rupture or infection (septic tenosynovitis).
  • Erythema and warmth - Suggests an inflammatory or infectious aetiology.
  • Systemic symptoms (fever, malaise) - Raises suspicion for infection or inflammatory arthritis.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Chronic painless dysfunction, often seen in older adults with longstanding tendinosis without acute inflammation.
  • Diffuse wrist pain without focal tenderness, mimicking other pathologies like carpal tunnel syndrome.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Ultrasound - Identifies tendon thickening, hypoechoic areas, or neovascularisation, correlating with tendinopathy.
  • MRI - Reveals tendon degeneration and adjacent soft tissue changes; useful in refractory cases.
  • Plain radiographs - Typically normal but may exclude bony abnormalities.
Problem Representation [sc name="ask-ai-110" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • A 40-year-old manual worker presents with 6 weeks of worsening wrist pain, particularly with gripping or lifting, accompanied by swelling and reduced range of motion.

Treatment [sc name="ask-ai-112" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Conservative management - Rest, splinting, and physiotherapy as first-line treatment.
  • Analgesics - NSAIDs for pain relief and to reduce inflammation in acute cases.
  • Injection therapy - Corticosteroid or platelet-rich plasma injections for refractory cases.
  • Surgery - Tendon debridement or repair for chronic or severe cases.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Wrist Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Chronic pain - Due to persistent degeneration and nerve sensitisation.
  • Tendon rupture - Rare but serious, resulting from untreated or severe tendinopathy.
  • Functional impairment - Long-term disability from reduced tendon elasticity and strength.
