  • Gilbertâ€™s syndrome is caused by mutations in the UGT1A1 gene, resulting in reduced activity of UDP-glucuronosyltransferase, which conjugates bilirubin in hepatocytes.

  • Because unconjugated bilirubin is less water-soluble, it accumulates in tissues, occasionally causing mild jaundice.
