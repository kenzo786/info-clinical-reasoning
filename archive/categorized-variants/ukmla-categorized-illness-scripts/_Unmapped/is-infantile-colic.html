Infantile Colic
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Infantile Colic"][/sc]

Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Infantile Colic"][/sc]
  • While the exact cause is unknown, proposed mechanisms include gastrointestinal discomfort (e.g., gas, immaturity of gut motility), hypersensitivity to stimuli, or dysbiosis in gut microbiota. Neurodevelopmental immaturity may contribute to difficulties in self-regulation.
  • Excessive crying may result in swallowing air, exacerbating gastrointestinal symptoms.

Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Infantile Colic"][/sc]
  • Excessive crying - Crying episodes exceeding 3 hours per day; thought to result from discomfort or sensory overload.
  • Fussiness - Periods of irritability unrelated to hunger or nappy changes, potentially linked to gut motility issues.
  • Difficulty soothing - Crying often unresponsive to usual soothing techniques, attributed to neurodevelopmental immaturity.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Infantile Colic"][/sc]
  • Tensed abdomen - Indicates gastrointestinal discomfort due to gas or bloating.
  • Flexed legs - Often seen during crying episodes, reflecting a pain response in the abdomen.
  • Normal growth and development - Confirms the absence of an underlying pathological condition.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Infantile Colic"][/sc]
  • Failure to thrive - Suggests an underlying condition such as malabsorption or metabolic disorder.
  • Fever - May indicate infection, requiring further evaluation.

Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Infantile Colic"][/sc]
  • Prolonged duration - Crying persisting beyond 4 months of age, possibly indicating an alternative diagnosis like reflux or neurological conditions.
  • Intermittent severe episodes - May be misdiagnosed as colic but represent conditions such as intussusception.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Infantile Colic"][/sc]

  • Parental education - Reassure parents about the self-limiting nature of the condition and normalise their experience.

  • Over-medicalisation - Unnecessary investigations or treatments due to misdiagnosis.
