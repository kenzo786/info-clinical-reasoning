Food Poisoning
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Food Poisoning"][/sc]
  • Food poisoning is an acute gastrointestinal illness caused by ingestion of contaminated food or water. It results from toxins produced by bacteria, viruses, parasites, or chemicals and leads to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, and abdominal pain. The condition is typically self-limiting but can occasionally result in severe complications, especially in vulnerable populations such as young children, the elderly, and immunocompromised individuals.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Food Poisoning"][/sc]
  • UK GP prevalence: In the UK, there are approximately 500,000 cases of food poisoning annually, with a majority managed in primary care.
  • UK hospital prevalence: Roughly 20,000 cases of food poisoning lead to hospital admissions each year.
  • Worldwide prevalence: Globally, an estimated 600 million people fall ill from foodborne diseases annually, with around 420,000 deaths, according to WHO estimates.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Food Poisoning"][/sc]
  • 1. Contaminated food or water introduces pathogens or toxins into the gastrointestinal tract.
  • 2. The pathogen may adhere to the intestinal lining or produce enterotoxins, causing local irritation and inflammation.
  • 3. Toxins or pathogens increase intestinal secretion of electrolytes and fluids, leading to diarrhoea.
  • 4. Some organisms invade intestinal mucosa, causing direct tissue damage and a systemic inflammatory response (fever, malaise).
  • 5. The immune system responds with neutrophilic infiltration, which further exacerbates inflammation and fluid loss.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Food Poisoning"][/sc]
  • Incubation period: The time from ingestion to symptom onset varies from a few hours (e.g., Staphylococcus aureus) to a few days (e.g., Salmonella or Campylobacter).
  • Acute phase: Symptoms such as diarrhoea, vomiting, and abdominal pain typically last 1â€“3 days.
  • Recovery phase: Most cases resolve within a week without complications, but fatigue and mild gastrointestinal symptoms may persist for several more days.
