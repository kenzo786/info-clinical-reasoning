Raised Intracranial Pressure (ICP)
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Raised Intracranial Pressure (ICP)"][/sc]
  • Raised intracranial pressure (ICP) is a pathological increase in the pressure within the skull, which can occur due to increased brain volume (e.g. oedema, mass lesions), increased cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) volume (e.g. hydrocephalus), or increased blood volume (e.g. venous outflow obstruction). It can lead to brain herniation and death if not treated promptly.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Raised Intracranial Pressure (ICP)"][/sc]
  • In the UK, raised ICP is relatively uncommon in general practice but can be seen in hospitalised patients with trauma, brain tumours, or hydrocephalus. Global estimates vary depending on the underlying cause, but it is common in patients with traumatic brain injury (TBI), affecting up to 60% of severe cases.
