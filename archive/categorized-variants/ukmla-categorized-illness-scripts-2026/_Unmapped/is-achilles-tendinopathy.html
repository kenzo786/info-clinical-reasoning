Achilles Tendinopathy
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Achilles tendinopathy is a chronic overuse injury of the Achilles tendon characterised by pain, stiffness, and impaired function. It commonly occurs due to repeated microtrauma and inadequate healing, affecting the midportion of the tendon or the insertion at the calcaneus.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • [sc name="1095-jitl-epidemiology-link" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • UK GP prevalence: ~0.2% of consultations annually, higher in active populations.
  • Hospital prevalence: Frequently encountered in sports medicine and orthopaedic clinics.
  • Worldwide prevalence: Common in physically active populations, with a lifetime risk of 52% in elite runners.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Repeated mechanical loading causes microtears in the Achilles tendon.
  • Disruption of normal collagen structure and accumulation of disorganised Type III collagen.
  • Neovascularisation and nerve ingrowth contribute to pain.
  • Chronic inflammation leads to a degenerative state known as tendinosis.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Acute phase: Sudden onset of pain and stiffness, often after increased activity.
  • Subacute phase: Pain and stiffness persist, typically worsening with activity.
  • Chronic phase: Symptoms present for >3 months, including persistent pain and functional impairment.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Localised pain in the Achilles region - Often worse in the morning or after periods of rest, due to tendon stiffness and increased pressure sensitivity.
  • Stiffness - Particularly pronounced after inactivity, caused by reduced elasticity of the degenerated tendon.
  • Swelling or thickening - Indicates chronic degenerative changes and tendon repair processes.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Palpable tenderness - Localised tenderness over the Achilles tendon indicates inflammation or degeneration.
  • Crepitus - A grating sensation on palpation may indicate chronic damage or thickened paratenon.
  • Decreased range of motion - Limited dorsiflexion due to stiffness or pain.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Sudden severe pain and inability to bear weight - Suggests Achilles tendon rupture, a surgical emergency.
  • Associated systemic symptoms - Fever or erythema may indicate infection or inflammatory disease.
  • Progressive weakness - Could signal neurological or systemic involvement.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Non-painful swelling - Rare, may be confused with bursitis or cystic lesions.
  • Bilateral symptoms - Could indicate systemic inflammatory diseases such as rheumatoid arthritis.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Ultrasound - Shows tendon thickening, hypoechoic areas, and neovascularisation.
  • MRI - Detects tendon degeneration and fluid accumulation.
  • Blood tests - Rule out systemic causes if bilateral or with systemic symptoms (e.g., inflammatory markers).
Problem Representation [sc name="ask-ai-110" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • A 40-year-old male presents with a 3-month history of progressive posterior ankle pain and stiffness, worse in the morning and after running.
Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Plantar fasciitis - Pain localised to the heel, worse on first steps in the morning.
  • Achilles tendon rupture - Acute onset with a "pop" sound, inability to plantar flex.
  • Retrocalcaneal bursitis - Pain anterior to the tendon, with swelling and warmth.
Treatment [sc name="ask-ai-112" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Conservative - Eccentric loading exercises, physiotherapy, and NSAIDs for symptom relief.
  • Interventional - Corticosteroid injections (cautiously) or platelet-rich plasma therapy for refractory cases.
  • Surgical - Reserved for cases with severe degeneration or rupture unresponsive to conservative measures.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Achilles Tendinopathy"][/sc]
  • Tendon rupture - Often due to ongoing degeneration and overuse.
  • Chronic pain - Persistent symptoms can lead to significant functional impairment.
