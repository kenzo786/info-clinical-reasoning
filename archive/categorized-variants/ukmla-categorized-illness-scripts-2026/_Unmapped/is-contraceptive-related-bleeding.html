Contraceptive-Related Bleeding
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • Contraceptive-related bleeding refers to abnormal uterine bleeding associated with the use of hormonal contraceptive methods, including combined oral contraceptives (COCs), progestogen-only pills (POPs), intrauterine systems (IUS), injectables, and implants. It may present as irregular, prolonged, intermenstrual, or breakthrough bleeding.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • [sc name="1095-jitl-epidemiology-link" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • Common among users of hormonal contraception, affecting up to 30-50% of new users.
  • In UK primary care, it is a frequent reason for contraceptive discontinuation.
  • Worldwide, irregular bleeding is a leading cause of discontinuation of long-acting reversible contraception (LARC).
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • Endometrial atrophy: Progestogen-only methods can cause thinning of the endometrium, leading to unpredictable shedding.
  • Breakthrough bleeding: In COC users, insufficient oestrogen support may lead to unstable endometrial vasculature.
  • Progestogen effect: Prolonged exposure to progestogens in LARCs can lead to altered vascular remodelling and inflammatory changes.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • Most cases occur within the first 3-6 months of starting a new contraceptive method.
  • Bleeding patterns may stabilise with continued use.
  • Unpredictable bleeding may persist in some women, particularly with progestogen-only methods.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • Irregular bleeding - Most common, due to endometrial instability.
  • Prolonged bleeding - More frequent in progestogen-only methods, due to persistent endometrial breakdown.
  • Amenorrhoea - Seen with some progestogen methods, related to endometrial atrophy.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • No systemic signs - Typically, no physical findings apart from bleeding pattern changes.
  • Pelvic tenderness - If associated infection or structural pathology coexists.
  • Cervical ectropion - Common in COC users, can contribute to post-coital bleeding.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • Post-coital bleeding - May indicate cervical pathology such as cervical cancer.
  • Intermenstrual bleeding after 3-6 months - Needs exclusion of endometrial pathology.
  • Heavy or persistent bleeding - Consider underlying gynaecological conditions or bleeding disorders.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • Severe pelvic pain - May suggest an undiagnosed ectopic pregnancy.
  • Haemodynamic instability - Suggests significant blood loss, unusual in contraceptive-related bleeding.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • Pregnancy test - To rule out pregnancy-related bleeding.
  • STI screening - Particularly in younger or at-risk individuals.
  • Pelvic ultrasound - If structural pathology is suspected.
Problem Representation [sc name="ask-ai-110" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • A 25-year-old woman presents with irregular bleeding over the last three months after starting the progestogen-only pill. No associated pain, normal examination.
Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • Pregnancy-related bleeding - Distinguish with pregnancy test.
  • Endometrial pathology (polyps, hyperplasia) - Consider in persistent bleeding.
  • STI-related cervicitis - Screen for infection in at-risk groups.
Treatment [sc name="ask-ai-112" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • Reassurance - If within first 3-6 months of contraceptive use.
  • NSAIDs or Tranexamic acid - For heavy bleeding.
  • Change contraceptive method - If persistent bleeding is unacceptable to the patient.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Contraceptive-Related Bleeding"][/sc]
  • Discontinuation of contraception - Common due to bleeding inconvenience.
  • Unintended pregnancy - Due to poor compliance after stopping contraception.
