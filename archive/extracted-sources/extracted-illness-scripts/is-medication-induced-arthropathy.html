Medication-Induced Arthropathy
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Medication-Induced Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Medication-induced arthropathy refers to joint pain, swelling, or other articular symptoms caused by adverse effects of medications. It often involves inflammatory or mechanical changes in the joints, triggered by direct drug effects, metabolic alterations, or immune-mediated mechanisms.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Medication-Induced Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Joint Pain - Typically affecting large joints such as the knees or hips, caused by inflammation or cartilage degradation induced by the medication.
  • Swelling - Resulting from synovitis or effusion due to inflammatory mediators or immune complexes triggered by the drug.
  • Stiffness - Often worse in the morning, associated with inflammatory processes within the joint capsule.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Medication-Induced Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Joint Effusion - Observable swelling in the joint caused by excess synovial fluid due to drug-induced inflammation.
  • Reduced Range of Motion - Limited movement due to pain or joint capsule involvement.
  • Tenderness - On palpation of affected joints, indicating active inflammation or tissue damage.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Medication-Induced Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Severe Pain with Fever - May indicate septic arthritis as a complication of immune suppression due to medication.
  • Rapid Joint Destruction - Seen in cases with potent immunosuppressive drugs, necessitating urgent evaluation.
  • Systemic Symptoms - Such as rash, malaise, or organ involvement, suggesting a drug-induced systemic autoimmune response (e.g., lupus-like syndrome).
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Medication-Induced Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Monoarthritis - Instead of the more common polyarthritis, caused by specific drug localisation effects.
  • Painless Swelling - Particularly in corticosteroid-related cases, due to metabolic effects rather than inflammation.
  • Delayed Onset - Symptoms may appear weeks to months after starting the medication, obscuring the link.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Medication-Induced Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Blood Tests - Raised inflammatory markers (e.g., CRP, ESR) in inflammatory cases; specific autoantibodies in lupus-like syndrome.
  • Joint Aspiration - Analysis of synovial fluid to rule out infection or crystal-induced arthropathy.
  • Imaging - X-rays may show joint space narrowing or cartilage loss; MRI can detect early inflammatory changes.
Problem Representation [sc name="ask-ai-110" Q="Medication-Induced Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • A 55-year-old woman presenting with 3 months of progressive knee pain and swelling after starting a bisphosphonate, with morning stiffness lasting 30 minutes and no fever.
Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Medication-Induced Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Osteoarthritis - Gradual onset, worsens with activity, no significant inflammation markers.
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis - Symmetrical joint involvement, systemic features, positive rheumatoid factor or anti-CCP antibodies.
  • Septic Arthritis - Acute, often monoarticular, fever, positive joint fluid culture.
Treatment [sc name="ask-ai-112" Q="Medication-Induced Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Discontinuation of Offending Drug - Primary treatment step where feasible.
  • Symptomatic Management - NSAIDs for inflammation, corticosteroids for severe immune reactions.
  • Alternative Medications - Replace causative drugs with safer alternatives if possible.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Medication-Induced Arthropathy"][/sc]
  • Chronic Joint Damage - Due to prolonged inflammation or delayed diagnosis.
  • Systemic Autoimmune Disorders - Persistent drug-induced lupus or vasculitis.
  • Reduced Mobility - From untreated symptoms leading to joint stiffness and dysfunction.
