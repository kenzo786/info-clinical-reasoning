Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Sprains: Injuries to ligaments, which are bands of fibrous tissue connecting bones. They occur when a ligament is stretched or torn due to excessive force.
  • Strains: Injuries to muscles or tendons, which are fibrous tissues attaching muscles to bones. Strains result from overstretching, tearing, or overuse of the muscle or tendon.
  • Both conditions can range in severity from mild overstretching to complete tears and are common in athletic or physically active individuals.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • UK GP population: Sprains and strains account for approximately 7-10% of musculoskeletal consultations.
  • UK hospital prevalence: Represent up to 40% of emergency department presentations related to musculoskeletal injuries.
  • Worldwide prevalence: Variable based on activity levels; significantly higher in sports-active populations, with rates up to 20-30% annually in athletes.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Sprains: Excessive force leads to ligament fibre disruption, causing inflammation, pain, and loss of joint stability.
  • Strains: Overstretching or repetitive microtrauma leads to muscle fibre or tendon damage, with inflammation, local bleeding, and reduced contractile strength.
  • Overuse injuries: Chronic microtrauma due to repetitive motion or stress exceeds the tissue's repair capacity, leading to progressive weakening and inflammation.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Acute phase (1-3 days): Pain, swelling, and limited mobility due to inflammation and tissue damage.
  • Subacute phase (4-14 days): Early tissue repair with gradual reduction in swelling and return of function.
  • Chronic phase (weeks to months): Persistent symptoms may occur in severe injuries or with overuse conditions due to inadequate healing.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Pain: Localised to the affected area, often exacerbated by movement or pressure. Results from inflammation and nerve irritation.
  • Swelling: Due to vascular leakage and inflammatory mediators at the injury site.
  • Bruising: Visible skin discolouration due to bleeding under the skin from damaged blood vessels.
  • Stiffness: Reduced range of motion secondary to pain and swelling limiting joint or muscle movement.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Tenderness: Localised pain on palpation due to tissue damage and inflammation.
  • Swelling: Observable oedema in the affected area from fluid accumulation.
  • Reduced strength: Weakness on testing, indicating muscle or tendon damage.
  • Instability (sprains): Excessive joint movement due to ligament damage, particularly in the ankle or knee.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Severe swelling and bruising: May indicate a complete tear or associated fracture.
  • Inability to bear weight: Suggests severe injury, such as a complete ligament tear or bony injury.
  • Persistent instability: Indicates chronic ligament insufficiency requiring further intervention.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Minimal swelling or pain: May occur in high-grade sprains with nerve disruption or chronic overuse injuries.
  • Delayed symptoms: Symptoms appearing after repetitive activity may suggest a stress-related overuse condition.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • X-ray: To exclude fractures, especially in high-risk injuries like ankle sprains.
  • MRI: Detailed imaging for soft tissue injuries such as ligament tears or muscle strains.
  • Ultrasound: Useful for tendon injuries and assessing joint effusion.
Problem Representation [sc name="ask-ai-110" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]

A 25-year-old male presents with acute ankle pain and swelling after twisting his ankle during football. He is unable to bear weight and reports localised tenderness over the lateral aspect.

Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Fracture: More severe pain, deformity, and inability to bear weight.
  • Tendon rupture: Palpable gap in the tendon, inability to activate the affected muscle.
  • Contusion: Localised bruising with minimal functional impairment.
Treatment [sc name="ask-ai-112" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation (RICE): Acute phase management to reduce inflammation and swelling.
  • Analgesia: NSAIDs such as ibuprofen for pain relief and inflammation control.
  • Physiotherapy: Rehabilitation for restoring strength, flexibility, and function.
  • Surgery: Reserved for complete tears or persistent instability.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Sprains and Strains (Including Overuse)"][/sc]
  • Chronic instability: Recurrent injuries due to unresolved ligament damage.
  • Osteoarthritis: Long-term consequence of joint instability or poor rehabilitation.
  • Tendonitis: Overuse injuries progressing to chronic tendon inflammation.
