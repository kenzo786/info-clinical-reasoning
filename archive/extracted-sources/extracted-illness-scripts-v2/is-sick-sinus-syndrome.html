Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • Sick sinus syndrome (SSS) is a group of heart rhythm disorders involving dysfunction of the sinus node, the heart’s natural pacemaker. It leads to abnormal heart rhythms, including sinus bradycardia, tachy-brady syndrome, sinus pauses, and atrial fibrillation. The condition often results in a slow heart rate (bradycardia), but it can also present with alternating fast and slow rhythms.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • Prevalence in the UK GP population: SSS is relatively uncommon, affecting around 1 in 600 patients over the age of 65.
  • UK hospital prevalence: SSS accounts for up to 50% of cases leading to pacemaker implantation in the UK.
  • Worldwide prevalence: Global incidence varies but is primarily a condition of older adults, with increasing prevalence in populations over 65 years old.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • The sinus node (SA node) generates electrical impulses that control the heart rate. In SSS, the SA node malfunctions due to fibrosis, ischaemia, or intrinsic degeneration, leading to impaired impulse formation or conduction. This can result in sinus bradycardia, sinus pauses, or alternating bradycardia and tachycardia (tachy-brady syndrome).
  • Fibrosis of the sinoatrial node is the most common cause, reducing the node’s ability to generate impulses, which leads to bradycardia. Tachycardia can occur due to compensatory mechanisms or accompanying atrial fibrillation.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • SSS typically develops slowly, with intermittent symptoms. Early stages may be asymptomatic, but over time, symptoms such as fatigue and dizziness increase in frequency as bradycardia or tachy-brady episodes become more severe. Episodes may be transient, with long asymptomatic periods between them.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • Fatigue - Common due to insufficient cardiac output during bradycardia, reducing oxygen delivery to tissues.
  • Dizziness or light-headedness - A result of decreased cerebral perfusion during prolonged bradycardic episodes.
  • Syncope or near-syncope - Occurs with severe bradycardia or sinus pauses, leading to significant hypotension and transient loss of consciousness.
  • Palpitations - Often present in patients with tachy-brady syndrome, where tachycardia or atrial fibrillation follows bradycardia.
  • Chest pain or discomfort - May occur due to ischaemia during periods of tachycardia or bradycardia.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • Bradycardia - A slow heart rate (< 60 bpm) on examination, often persistent in SSS.
  • Tachycardia - Alternating with bradycardia, especially in tachy-brady syndrome.
  • Irregular pulse - Seen with atrial fibrillation or ectopic beats in patients with tachy-brady syndrome.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • Syncope - Indicates significant pauses in cardiac rhythm or severe bradycardia, increasing the risk of falls or sudden cardiac arrest.
  • Severe fatigue or dyspnoea - May signify advanced heart failure due to prolonged periods of bradycardia.
  • Chest pain - Suggests myocardial ischaemia during bradycardia or tachyarrhythmias, potentially leading to myocardial infarction.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • Asymptomatic bradycardia - Especially in older adults, SSS may present without symptoms but with persistent bradycardia noted on routine examination or ECG.
  • Intermittent dizziness or palpitations - Episodes may be misattributed to anxiety or other non-cardiac causes, leading to a delay in diagnosis.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • ECG - May show sinus bradycardia, sinus pauses, or tachy-brady syndrome. In atrial fibrillation, an irregular rhythm is seen.
  • 24-hour Holter monitoring - Helps capture intermittent episodes of bradycardia, tachycardia, or sinus pauses that are not present on a single ECG.
  • Electrophysiological studies (EPS) - May be used to evaluate sinus node function, particularly if non-invasive tests are inconclusive.
Problem Representation [sc name="ask-ai-110" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • A 75-year-old female presents with episodes of dizziness, fatigue, and palpitations over several months. ECG shows sinus bradycardia alternating with atrial fibrillation (tachy-brady syndrome), and Holter monitoring reveals frequent sinus pauses.
Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • Atrial fibrillation - AF presents with an irregularly irregular rhythm but lacks the sinus pauses or bradycardia seen in SSS.
  • Vasovagal syncope - Causes dizziness and syncope but typically involves a trigger (e.g., stress, pain) and lacks ECG abnormalities.
  • Heart block - Particularly 2nd or 3rd degree block can present with bradycardia, but with clear atrioventricular (AV) dissociation on ECG.
Treatment [sc name="ask-ai-112" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • Pacemaker implantation - The mainstay of treatment for symptomatic bradycardia or tachy-brady syndrome. A dual-chamber pacemaker is commonly used.
  • Rate control for atrial fibrillation - Beta-blockers or calcium channel blockers are used to control rapid ventricular rates in AF, but with caution in patients with bradycardia.
  • Anticoagulation - In patients with atrial fibrillation, anticoagulation (e.g., warfarin or DOACs) is recommended based on CHA2DS2-VASc score to reduce stroke risk.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Sick Sinus Syndrome (SSS)"][/sc]
  • Heart failure - Due to chronically slow heart rate or tachycardia, leading to reduced cardiac output and ventricular dysfunction.
  • Stroke - Associated with atrial fibrillation and the formation of thrombi in the atria, increasing the risk of embolic events.
  • Sudden cardiac arrest - Rare but can occur due to prolonged sinus pauses or severe bradycardia.
