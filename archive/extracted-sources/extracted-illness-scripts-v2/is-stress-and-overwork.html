Stress and Overwork
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • Stress and overwork refer to a state of mental and physical strain resulting from excessive workplace demands, leading to burnout, emotional exhaustion, and physical symptoms. Chronic exposure to stress without adequate recovery can impact multiple bodily systems, with consequences for mental health, cardiovascular health, and overall functioning.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • In the UK, stress is a leading cause of sick leave, affecting around 828,000 workers (2019/20) and contributing to over half of all workplace-related ill health.
  • High prevalence globally, particularly in occupations with high demands and low control (e.g., healthcare, finance, IT).
  • World Health Organisation identifies stress as a growing occupational health concern worldwide.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • Chronic stress activates the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, increasing cortisol and adrenaline release, which initially helps the body cope with acute demands but, over time, leads to adverse effects.
  • Excess cortisol affects various organs, weakening the immune response, impairing digestion, causing hyperglycaemia, and disrupting sleep.
  • Chronic activation of the HPA axis contributes to systemic inflammation, cardiovascular strain, and mental health effects, particularly anxiety and depression.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • **Acute Phase:** Increased alertness, temporary energy surge (hours to days).
  • **Chronic Phase:** Persistent symptoms like fatigue, irritability, and concentration issues (weeks to months).
  • **Burnout Phase:** Severe exhaustion, detachment, and decreased performance (months to years without intervention).
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • Fatigue - Persistent exhaustion due to the body’s prolonged cortisol and adrenaline release, leading to energy depletion.
  • Difficulty Concentrating - Impaired cognitive function from cortisol’s effect on the hippocampus and prefrontal cortex, which manage memory and focus.
  • Irritability - Elevated cortisol and adrenaline can heighten emotional responses, causing mood swings.
  • Sleep Disturbance - High cortisol levels disrupt circadian rhythms, impacting sleep onset and quality.
  • Physical Aches (headache, muscle tension) - Persistent muscle tension due to stress hormones can cause pain, commonly in the neck and shoulders.
  • Appetite Changes - Cortisol affects appetite regulation, leading to overeating or appetite loss.
  • Increased Susceptibility to Illness - Chronic cortisol suppresses immune function, making infections more common.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • Tachycardia - Increased heart rate due to heightened adrenaline and sympathetic nervous system activation.
  • Hypertension - Elevated blood pressure from chronic sympathetic activation, which can cause arterial stiffness over time.
  • Increased Sweating - Resulting from adrenaline’s effect on sweat glands, particularly during stressful situations.
  • Weight Fluctuations - Stress can lead to weight gain (due to cortisol’s effect on fat storage) or weight loss (from decreased appetite).
  • Eye Bags or Dark Circles - Persistent lack of sleep affects skin quality, causing dark circles.
Red Flag Features [sc name="ask-ai-107" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • Severe Chest Pain - May indicate cardiovascular events due to prolonged hypertension and stress; warrants urgent investigation.
  • Suicidal Ideation - Chronic stress is a risk factor for depression, and suicidal thoughts require immediate mental health intervention.
  • Significant Weight Loss - Suggests severe stress affecting nutrition and immune response; may require further assessment.
Atypical Presentations [sc name="ask-ai-108" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • Gastrointestinal Issues - Chronic stress may present primarily with irritable bowel symptoms like diarrhoea or bloating due to altered gut motility and microbiota changes.
  • Cardiovascular Symptoms in Young Adults - Symptoms such as palpitations and chest pain due to stress are sometimes misdiagnosed as panic disorder or cardiac issues in young adults.
Diagnostic Tests [sc name="ask-ai-109" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • Serum Cortisol Levels - May be elevated in chronic stress, though testing is usually reserved for complex cases.
  • Full Blood Count - Helps rule out anaemia or infection in cases of fatigue and frequent illness.
  • Electrocardiogram (ECG) - To exclude cardiovascular causes if palpitations or chest pain are present, as chronic stress can exacerbate heart disease.
Problem Representation [sc name="ask-ai-110" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • A 45-year-old woman presents with six months of persistent fatigue, sleep disturbances, and frequent headaches. She reports high workplace stress and no significant prior mental health issues. Her blood pressure is slightly elevated, and she struggles to concentrate on daily tasks.
Differential Diagnosis [sc name="ask-ai-111" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • Depression - While overlapping with stress, depression often involves low mood, feelings of hopelessness, and may lack a clear external stressor.
  • Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (CFS) - CFS presents with severe fatigue, unrefreshing sleep, and muscle pain, with no clear external stress trigger.
  • Generalised Anxiety Disorder (GAD) - GAD involves pervasive worry across multiple domains, not limited to workplace stress, with physical symptoms like tension and palpitations.
Treatment [sc name="ask-ai-112" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) - Recommended by NICE guidelines for stress management, CBT helps manage thought patterns contributing to stress.
  • Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction - Effective for managing stress, reducing cortisol levels, and enhancing emotional regulation.
  • Pharmacotherapy - SSRIs may be prescribed if stress is associated with anxiety or depressive symptoms.
  • Lifestyle Changes - Regular exercise, structured relaxation, and sleep hygiene can reduce symptoms significantly.
Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Stress and Overwork"][/sc]
  • Cardiovascular Disease - Chronic stress increases hypertension risk, which may lead to cardiovascular complications over time.
  • Depression and Anxiety Disorders - Unmanaged stress can lead to long-term mental health conditions requiring treatment.
  • Metabolic Issues - Elevated cortisol can contribute to insulin resistance and obesity in chronic stress.
  • Impaired Immune Function - Chronic stress reduces immunity, increasing the risk for infections.
