Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD)"][/sc]
  • Metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD) is a chronic liver condition characterised by hepatic steatosis (fat accumulation in the liver) in individuals with metabolic risk factors, in the absence of significant alcohol consumption or other secondary causes.
  • UKMLA learning outcome: Recognise the clinical features, risk factors, and potential complications of MASLD, including its progression to more advanced liver disease.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD)"][/sc]
  • [sc name="1095-jitl-epidemiology-link" Q="Metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD)"][/sc]
  • MASLD is highly prevalent in the UK, affecting approximately 20-30% of the adult population, with a higher incidence in individuals with obesity, type 2 diabetes, and metabolic syndrome (NICE).
  • Risk factors include central obesity, insulin resistance, dyslipidaemia, hypertension, and type 2 diabetes, often clustering as metabolic syndrome.
  • The prevalence of MASLD is increasing in line with rising rates of obesity and type 2 diabetes, particularly in younger age groups.
