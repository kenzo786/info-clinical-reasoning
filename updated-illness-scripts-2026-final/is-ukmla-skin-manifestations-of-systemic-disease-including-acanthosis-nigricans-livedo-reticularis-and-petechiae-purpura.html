Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Skin manifestations of systemic disease (including acanthosis nigricans, livedo reticularis and petechiae/ purpura)"][/sc]
  • Check for acanthosis nigricans (velvety hyperpigmentation, typically in skin folds) - implies insulin resistance or malignancy.
  • Check for livedo reticularis (mottled, reticulated vascular pattern) - suggests vasculopathy or hypercoagulable states.
  • Check for petechiae/purpura (non-blanching red/purple spots) - indicates bleeding diathesis, vasculitis, or thrombocytopenia.
  • Check vital signs - fever, tachycardia, or hypotension may indicate systemic inflammation or sepsis.

Complications [sc name="ask-ai-115" Q="Skin manifestations of systemic disease (including acanthosis nigricans, livedo reticularis and petechiae/ purpura)"][/sc]
  • Progression of underlying systemic disease: Uncontrolled systemic illness can lead to severe organ damage and increased morbidity.
  • Psychological impact: Visible skin changes can cause significant distress, affecting quality of life and mental well-being.
