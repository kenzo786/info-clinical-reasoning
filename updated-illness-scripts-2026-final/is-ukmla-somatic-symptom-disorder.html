Somatic symptom disorder [sc name="ask-ai-100" Q="Somatic symptom disorder"][/sc]
Definition [sc name="ask-ai-101" Q="Somatic symptom disorder"][/sc]
  • A mental health condition characterised by physical symptoms causing significant distress or functional impairment, without a clear medical explanation, or with excessive thoughts, feelings, or behaviours related to the symptoms.
  • UKMLA learning outcome: Recognise somatic symptom disorder as a distinct clinical entity, differentiating it from other mental health conditions and medically unexplained symptoms.
Epidemiology [sc name="ask-ai-102" Q="Somatic symptom disorder"][/sc]
  • [sc name="1095-jitl-epidemiology-link" Q="Somatic symptom disorder"][/sc]
  • Prevalence in the UK is estimated to be around 5-7% in the general population, with higher rates in primary care settings [1].
  • Risk factors include female sex, lower socioeconomic status, history of childhood trauma, co-morbid anxiety or depression, and a lack of social support.
Pathophysiology [sc name="ask-ai-103" Q="Somatic symptom disorder"][/sc]
  • Trigger: Psychological distress or trauma may lead to heightened somatic awareness and misinterpretation of normal bodily sensations.
  • Progression: Central sensitisation and altered pain processing pathways in the brain contribute to the persistence and amplification of physical symptoms.
  • Consequence: A vicious cycle develops where physical symptoms lead to increased anxiety, further exacerbating symptoms and functional impairment.
Time Course [sc name="ask-ai-104" Q="Somatic symptom disorder"][/sc]
  • Early phase: Initial presentation of physical symptoms, often vague and multiple, leading to frequent medical consultations and investigations.
  • Progressive phase: Symptoms become more chronic and pervasive, particularly beyond 6 months, impacting daily functioning and quality of life.
  • Late phase: Established patterns of illness behaviour, often with significant disability and resistance to conventional medical interventions.
Symptoms [sc name="ask-ai-105" Q="Somatic symptom disorder"][/sc]
  • Ask about persistent pain - as it is a common and often debilitating symptom, leading to significant distress.
  • Ask about fatigue - as it can be profound and contribute to functional impairment, often without clear physical cause.
  • Ask about gastrointestinal complaints (e.g., abdominal pain, bloating) - as these are frequently reported and can be a focus of health anxiety.
  • Ask about neurological symptoms (e.g., weakness, numbness) - as these can be particularly distressing and lead to extensive investigations.
  • Ask about disproportionate thoughts and feelings about symptoms - this is a core diagnostic criterion, indicating psychological distress.
Signs [sc name="ask-ai-106" Q="Somatic symptom disorder"][/sc]
  • Check vital signs - typically normal, which helps rule out acute medical instability.
  • Check general appearance - may show signs of distress or preoccupation with symptoms, but often no specific physical findings.
  • Check for tenderness or non-anatomical sensory changes - these can be inconsistent or widespread, not fitting typical neurological patterns.
  • Check for signs of anxiety or depression - often co-exist and can exacerbate somatic symptoms.
